# Conversations
### Adam

The creature hovers over me, its eyes wide, lips pressed together in a thin line. Its shoulder moves in a repetitive motion, causing me to scream.

Why am I screaming?

What is it doing to me? My howls appear to echo, but the creature gives them no mind. As it repeats its motions, its long hair undulates in time with the movement, causing me to suffer.

The high-pitched sound of an Atomizer loading next to my ear wakes me. I can feel the cold metal on my temple before I open my eyes, aware of what is happening.

A man’s voice rings in my ear. “Do not turn around.”

The voice sounds familiar, but I am drunk with sleep and unable to match a face to the voice.

“I understand. What is it that you want?”

“This is not about what I want. What do you want?”

“To be free,” I answer, before I can realize the meaning of my words.

The Atomizer sinks deeper into my temple.

“Why should I believe you?”

I recognize the voice.

“Because I told you my name in the orchard.”

“You did more than that,” Sylvan says as he steps away from the bed. “You let Augustus go. Why? Why did you not call for backup?”

“I was curious.”

“Dangerous thing,” Sylvan says, “to be curious; a good way to get yourself killed.”

“Sometimes death is preferable.”

Sylvan sighs, holstering his Atomizer.

My eyes fixate on his weapon. “Where did you get that?”

Sylvan looks amused as he leans back on my kitchen counter, crossing his arms.

“A friend gave it to me.”

“What else can your friends do?”

“Depends, what do you need?”

“To leave this place.”

Sylvan seems lost in thought. Is he convinced?

“You know,” Sylvan continues, “we can really use a man with your skills.”

Sitting up quietly, I look at the man in my apartment, but say nothing.

“How do I know we can trust you?”

“Think back on our conversation in the orchard,” I reply.

“I have, and that is the only reason why I did not pull the trigger as you slept. I am inclined to trust you.”

“How can I prove myself?”

He hesitates before answering. “We’re planning a mission. If we succeed, this has the potential to shake the Protectorate to its very core.”

Sylvan takes a deep breath and looks at me. “What do you think?”

I consider his proposition for a moment. Despite the fact that I was promised a pardon for crimes committed during the infiltration, there must be a limit to what the mayor is willing to forgive.

If Sylvan is telling the truth, and they can indeed disrupt the workings of the Protectorate, having any role to play in that would certainly mean death.

On the other hand, if I refuse, I have a feeling that Sylvan would not hesitate to vaporize me before I could reach for my own Atomizer.

The best course of action is to play along for now and do my absolute best to minimize the damage.

“So you want to do more than simply escape?”

“Once outside,” Sylvan says, “we’ll be lost. The more information we have about what is up there, the better equipped we will be. Before we leave, this information will be disseminated to as many people as possible—our gift to the Protectorate.”

I think it over for a few moments. “I’m in.”

“Good,” Sylvan replies. “I’m happy you took some time to think about it. If you had answered yes immediately, my suspicion that you were a double-agent would have been confirmed, and you would be red mist.”

“I’m happy I did think about it then,” I say with a smile.

“Tonight, at the statue in the middle of Invictus Park. That’s where we’ll meet.”

“Who’s us?” I ask.

“The brothers who will put the plan into motion,” he says, “and you will play a crucial part.”

“We’re doing it tonight?”

“Does that change your mind?” Sylvan asks.

“No. I’ll be there.”

“Good, bring your Atomizer and wristwatch,” he says as he opens the door to my apartment and walks out.

I collapse back onto my bed. What should I do?

If I report this to the mayor, the members in attendance would be arrested, but I have a feeling that there were many more who would not be there. This would only see a handful of people arrested, at the cost of my cover being blown, and making any other future operations far more difficult.

Should I at least contact the mayor and get him up to date?

As I reach for my watch, I change my mind.

That V character is likely monitoring all of my communications. Calling the mayor right after our unscheduled meeting would be ill-advised.

Is this really the reason why I hesitate? Is it because I’m afraid I’ll blow my cover, and that the arrest won’t have a wide enough impact? Or is there a different reason, maybe one which I am unwilling to even admit to myself?

Shaking my head, I banish the thought. My loyalty lies with the Protectorate and the Praetorians.

There is no choice to be made. I have to keep the mayor in the dark for now. By the end of my mission, assuming they don’t double-cross me first, I’ll try to figure out how they are to obtain information about the outside world.

If it’s something dangerous that can actually destroy the Protectorate, I would have to blow my cover and try to arrest the members that I do know about.

However, what if things do not go to plan? What if I can not figure out what their goal is in time? Assuming they do try to double-cross me first, how would I react? What crimes would the mayor actually be willing to forgive, and which would he not?

My head hurts; too many variables and way too many potential outcomes.

I decide not to worry about it, and that I should get some more rest if I am to perform well this evening. With that thought still ringing in my mind, I drift back to sleep.
