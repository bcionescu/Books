# Weakness
### Ambrosia

“Hello, sweetie,” says Grunwald, baring his teeth at me, as he wipes blood from his hands.

“Say,” he continues, gabbing my wrist, “why don’t you show me some attention too, ey?”

“I am here on official duty,” I say, not looking at him. “Do you wish to interfere?”

He releases my wrist, cursing, as I wait for another guard to open the cell block. I step through, heading towards the man I had met the day before.

Why has he been assigned to me again?

Arriving at the correct cell, I step through the force field, dropping my pouch in shock.

Adam is sitting up on his bed, looking at me. His face is covered in blood, as well as parts of his hair.

“Hello, Ambrosia,” he says.

I purse my lips, picking up the pouch, and sit next to him.

“What did they do to you?” I ask.

“It would seem that they had more questions.”

I open my pouch, pull out a cloth and my bottle of disinfectant, and begin cleaning his wounds once again. As my arm moves, his eyes fixate on something.

“What is that?” he asks, placing his hand on my arm, making me wince in pain. “A cut?”

His eyes dart to mine. “What happened?”

I look down at my feet as I speak, “They told me I took too long with you, so they had to teach me a lesson.”

He leaned back onto the wall, looking at me. I wonder if he thinks he’s at fault. I know that I am the only one to blame.

He reaches for my hand and grabs the cloth. I let go as my eyes turn towards him. Carefully lifting the short sleeve of my dress, he begins to dab my wound slowly.

“I’m sorry if this hurts,” he says.

My eyes open wide, unable to move from his face. Why is he doing this? What is he trying to gain from me? Does he feel bad? Does he pity me?

“If it helps, you can leave now. Just leave me the cloth, and I’ll clean my wounds,” he says, as he continues to work on mine.

“When they take me,” he continues, “I’ll hide it, so they won’t know. You can always come to retrieve it… later.”

He means when he’s dead. Why is he doing this? Why does he care?

I begin to like him, and I hate it.

What good is any of this? There is no escape; in a few days, I will go into a Life Tank, and he will likely be dead, either from torture or execution.

Despite my bleak thoughts, I can’t stop myself from allowing him to clean my cut. Why? Why do I feel so dizzy around him, so confused?

“No, it’s ok,” I say. “Your wounds are far more extensive this time, so I can justify staying longer.”

Why did I tell him I would stay longer?

“I’d like that,” he replies, smiling at me. “There, your cut should heal up nicely,” he says, handing me back the cloth.

I take it, unable to take my eyes off him.

“I believe it is now my turn?” he smiles.

I shake my head, wetting the cloth once more, and getting to work on his extensive wounds.

“So I’ve figured out how we’re born, back where I’m from,” he says.

“Oh?”

“We’re born of the Hosts,” Adam says. “My mother is likely one of them, though I do not know which.”

“I’m sorry to hear that,” I reply. “I do not know my mother either.”

“How so?”

“Soldiers, guards, dresses, and pretty much everyone else are born like that.”

“Is there another way to be born?” he asks.

“Well, I’m not sure, but I know the High Ones are born a different way. I don’t know exactly how, though.”

I look up at him. He seems lost in thought. I start working on his lips.

“Ouch,” he laughs.

“Sorry,” I murmur, catching myself smiling.

When did I laugh last? And why do I feel so strange around him?

“What happened?” I ask, as I carefully touch his lips with my fingers.

“I refused to answer questions,” he says. “Got a few punches for my trouble.”

“Grunwald?” I ask.

Adam nods.

I do not like this Grunwald character one bit. The way I caught him looking at me both times I came up here makes me uncomfortable, especially after he grabbed me earlier.

As I carefully examine his lip, I notice that he is looking at me.

“What?” I ask, blushing.

“I don’t know. You’re really the first woman I’ve ever met, and I feel rather strange around you.”

I see I’m not the only one.

“How so?” I ask, pretending to be aloof.

“Well, for one, I really like the feeling of you taking care of my wounds. I’ve never felt anything like this before, never been cared for.”

“You’ve never been taken care of when you were young?”

“I have, but never like this,” he says, continuing to gaze into my eyes.

I blush and hope he does not know what that means.

Out of the corner of my eye, I can see his hand slowly moving towards my face. I let him place his fingers on my lips, without saying a word.

“How do they feel?” I ask.

“Really good,” he says.

I can feel the pace of my own breathing increase as he feels the edges of my mouth.

I find myself leaning in towards him. What he said before got to me, how nobody had shown him this kind of kindness before.

I did not say anything at the time, but I had also never had anyone care for me, not like this, not like how he cleaned my wound and offered to get me out of trouble.

As I lean in closer, his head tilts sideways. I know that there is no way he has ever kissed anyone before. Neither have I, for that matter, but I had at least heard about it. Clearly, his instincts have taken over. Our mouths find each other, and we begin to kiss.

* * *

### Adam

I have never felt anything like this before. I feel like running, if only I could leave this cell; like shouting, if only I knew what.

Ambrosia, this beautiful woman, now lies in my arms.

Her warmth makes me dizzy.

A thought crosses my mind, and suddenly, I push her away from me.

“What’s wrong?” she asks.

“I’m afraid you might get punished for this,” I murmur.

“If I am to be punished again, it will be worth it,” she says, leaning back into me and kissing me.

After a while, we come up for air, Ambrosia resting her head on my chest.

“I hope your lip doesn’t hurt too badly,” she says sheepishly.

“It does, but it was worth it,” I reply, making her laugh.

She turns her head, her chin now resting on my chest. “I’m due to go in the Life Tank in a couple of days.”

I do not want her to go. I want her to stay in my arms and not have to go inside a medical device from which she would never emerge.

“I don’t want to,” she continues.

I have never felt like this before. A crucial aspect of human existence, partnership, and intimacy, has been stolen from me. Not only that, but I never even knew it existed. I feel angry towards the Protectorate, wondering what other joys had been ripped from me.

“We should run away from here, somewhere far, where they can’t find us,” I find myself saying.

She smiles, tucking her head back into me.

“How?” she asks.

“I escaped once, I can do it again,” I say. “First, I need a way out of this cell. I know that the guards use similar technology to the Protectorate. If I can knock one unconscious, his weapon and wristwatch would be our way out.”

“How come you can walk through the force field?” I ask.

“With this,” she says, holding up her index finger. “This ring allows the wearer to walk in and out. If you don’t have one, you can go in, but not out.”

“I see,” I say. “So that allows you to move freely. You can’t throw it through the force field either, as I assume inanimate objects can’t pass through, one way or another, unless they’re attached to a person.”

“Correct,” says Ambrosia. “And if I walk through, and reach out my arm, so that you can take the ring, the force field would slice it off the moment the ring is removed.”

I am lost in thought, unsure of what to do.

“What if I stole a ring?” she whispers.

“You would do that?”

“The new Imperatrix is to be crowned tonight, so security will be minimal around the dungeons, as most guards will be in attendance. That would be our best chance,” she says.

“If I do it,” she continues, “will you be able to get us out? Through the Palace, and into the wilderness?”

“I believe so,” I say. I have seen the Palace on my way up here, at least the dungeon wing. If the coronation provides enough of a distraction, we have a real chance.

“That means you would be risking your life,” I say.

“My life will be over in a couple of days anyway. If we are to die trying to escape, then so be it,” Ambrosia says, kissing me.

“I need to go. I will be back tonight with an extra ring, and we can make our escape.”

“I look forward to it,” I say, smiling.

She beams at me and leans back down to steal one more kiss before picking up her pouch, patting down her flowery dress, and walking back through the force field.

I lay back on the bed, happier than I have ever been.
