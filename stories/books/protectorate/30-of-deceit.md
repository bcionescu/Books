# Of Deceit
### Adam

I stare at the ceiling, unable to sleep, unsure of what is to come. We managed to get close, freedom only feet away, but my fall drew attention to us.

I know I am unlikely to survive this, and yet, I can’t help but run through the events over and over, while they are still fresh in my mind. A few hours earlier, Ambrosia entered my cell, risking her life to bring me a ring.

Thankfully, I had sufficient foresight to take Grunwald’s uniform, enabling us to slip past the guard who questioned me.

As I mentally approach the forest, no matter how many times I run through the events, I can’t help but look at the Imperatrix.

Why? Why was I so drawn to those events? Why did my mind insist on spectating? The Mater’s hands slowly lift the crown, hovering over the head of her daughter, before crowning her, and then, night turns into day; I fall, and we get arrested.

My eyes turn towards the guards outside my cell. There is no chance of escape now; they’ll make sure of it. Ambrosia will either die with me, or she’ll be placed in one of those tanks, lulled into a long sleep.

Ambrosia…

I try not to think of what will happen to her. They know she’s involved, as she’s the one who knocked Grunwald unconscious.

Will that monster question her? What will he do? I close my eyes, desperately trying to fall asleep, in an attempt to avoid those thoughts.

Suddenly, my eyes open wide, my attention being drawn by the two guards outside. The sound of footsteps has made them stiffen. It couldn’t possibly be the Mater; not at this hour.

I peer out of the window, the sun only beginning to rise over the horizon.

“Your Brightness,” says one of the guards, as they both bow.

“Leave us,” she says.

The guards get back up, hesitating as they look at her.

“My apologies, the Imperatrix has—”

“Leave us,” she snaps.

The two men look at each other and bow before they hurry out of the dungeons.

“You too,” she says to one other unseen guard, as their footsteps shuffle away.

As the Mater enters my cell, the headache returns. A high-pitched sound reverberates through my bones as she approaches my cot.

Her face is hidden by a black veil, but I can sense she is holding my gaze.

“Take this,” she whispers, her hand outstretched towards me.

My eyes move down from her face to her hand, as my heart skips a beat.

“A ring?” I whisper?

“There is no time. Take it.”

I reach out and grab it, placing it around my finger, as I follow the Mater out of the cell.

“Ambrosia—”

“I know.”

She leads me into another cell, as Ambrosia looks at us, bewildered.

“Your Brightness,” she says, as she drops to her knees.

“Get up, girl, there isn’t any time,” she says, as she shoves another ring in her hand. “Let’s go.”

Ambrosia looks up at her, and then at me, as she gets up. I reach out my hand, and she takes it, as we follow the Mater out of the cell and into the corridor.

I look at the woman leading us out, and I sense anxiety, sorrow, and a sense of loss. Are these her feelings, or merely mine?

As she leads us outside, my headache begins to subside, making way for clearer thought.

“Take this,” she says, as she shoves a weapon in my hand. It feels heavy. Checking its side, I see the switch. This is my Atomizer.

Why is she giving this to me? How does she know that I won’t try to take her hostage on my way out?

I sense the Mater smiling at me as she wraps my hand around it.

“I can’t join you any further,” she says. “I would draw too much attention. They would never expect you to try the same thing, hours later, now would they?”

I look down at my clothes, remembering that I’m still wearing Grunwald’s uniform. Is she saying what I think she’s saying?

She lifts her hand, indicating the edge of the forest, a red bridge dominating the landscape in the background. My eyes wander to it for a moment, having never noticed it before.

“Head there,” she says. “Deeper into the forest, you will find a vehicle. Take it, and drive far away from here.”

She grabs Ambrosia’s hand, looking at her through her veil. “Drive far away, and never look back.”

“Why are you doing this?” I ask.

The Mater lifts her veil, allowing me to see her face for the first time. She looks young, slightly older than Ambrosia, but I know this must be impossible.

Her eyes are red from crying as she takes my face in her palms, searching my soul.

“I wish we had more time,” she murmurs, her voice shaking. “How I wish we had more time. Go, before it’s too late. I’ll try my best to keep the guards at bay.”

I grab Ambrosia’s hand, and we begin to rush towards the edge of the forest.

“Walk, don’t run.”

As my eyes turn away, I hear her saying I love you, as she returns to the Palace. Looking over at Ambrosia, I see no reaction, meaning that she did not hear her.

Did she think that? Was that an echo of her mind? If so, why would she say that? Did she say that, or is this a side effect of my headache?

Halfway through the grounds, I look back; that is where I see them. Two guards are talking to the Mater, as they frantically point at us.

Can she buy us enough time?

“Should we run?” whispers Ambrosia, her voice anxious.

“I think the fact that we’re walking is the only thing preventing them from chasing us down,” I say, as I look towards the forest.

“We’re not close enough yet. We need to keep walking.”

“You, stop!” I hear a man cry behind me.

My head feels numb. I grab Ambrosia’s hand as we sprint towards the edge of the forest.

As we run through the field, her dress clings to her body as the soldiers and their mutts chase us.

Releasing her hand as we run, I turn my head, aiming my Atomizer at the nearest guard, and press the trigger. The unfortunate man freezes in midair for a split second before turning into a fine red mist, causing the others to swear and veer, trying to avoid the gore.

I curse, realizing the Atomizer is set to vaporize. There is no time to change the setting.

Now slightly ahead of Ambrosia, I look at her, a sense of deja vu echoing through my body, as she runs towards me, her mouth open, screaming my name; her arms outstretched.

I grab onto her hand, once again looking forward, as we continue our desperate gallop, our chests heaving, legs aching. I can see it, the vehicle is right where the Mater said it would be, hidden in the trees.

Without looking, I aim the Atomizer behind me, shooting aimlessly, managing to hit at least one more target.

As we reach the edge of the forest, I help Ambrosia get inside the vehicle, jumping into my own seat immediately after. Looking around in a frenzy, I press the ignition button. The vehicle produces a synthetic whirring sound.

“Come on, come on,” I say, looking at the guards through the window, as I attempt to turn on the engine. I had never driven a vehicle like this, but its controls look similar enough to the ones in the Protectorate.

As we begin to move, I grab onto the wheel, steering away from the Palace, into the woods.

That’s when it happens—the engine dies.

### Mater

Shaking, I type out the message and hit Send, as the door to my quarters breaks open.

“Your Brightness, I’m sorry,” says the guard, shaking. “I’m so sorry, but please step away from the Terminal. You are under arrest.”

I look at him, knowing that I have done what I could. My hands lift, allowing the guards to cuff me, before walking me to my cell.
