# Glistening Towers
### Mater

“I am pleased with your work,” says the Mater.

“Thanks. It is good to know that one’s work is appreciated,” replies the engineer.

Insolent worm, how dare you speak to me this way? If only I had the power to, I would cut you down where you stand.

Remembering my training, I take three quick, quiet breaths and steady my pulse.

“The two envoys were not able to figure out that their device had been successfully tampered with. Will the Protectorate engineers be able to spot the tampering?” I ask.

“It is unlikely, given their technological development. Their artificial intelligence technology is rather rudimentary, and even though they had rigged the device to blow in case of tampering, that was very easy to circumvent,” replies the engineer.

“I see. Thank you for your assistance.”

“You are most welcome,” replies the engineer, before turning his back on me and sitting at his terminal.

“You may go now,” he says, waving me off.

“Thank you once more,” I say through gritted teeth, before turning around and heading out of the lab. As the elevator takes me to the ground floor, I feel humiliated and helpless.

I was an Imperatrix, and now I am a Mater. Despite that, this insolent brat treats me like I am nothing, a simple servant and nothing more.

“Let’s go,” I snap at the guards waiting outside, as I exit the glistening white tower.

“Where would you like to go, your Brightness?” the guard asks.

“Back to the Palace.”

As the armored vehicle slips through the empty streets, I mindlessly look out of the window at the people passing by. So small and insignificant. I could crush any of them at a moment’s notice, and yet, all of that power counts for nothing.

Our enemies surround us, and with all of my power, I must grovel at their feet, begging for scraps, never knowing why they do help when they choose to.

Their motives are opaque to me, even with my gifts, and this is a humiliation that I’ve had to accept my whole life.

As the vehicle comes to a stop in the courtyard, the guards step outside and open the door for me.

“Where is my daughter, Katherine?” I ask.

“The Imperatrix, daughter of the Sun, is in the garden, your Brightness.”

“Take me to her.”

### Imperatrix

I allow the sun to bathe me in its rays, as I playfully kick my feet back and forth. Soon I will have a child, my way, a successful rebellion, no matter how small, against a system that has given me no choice.

“The timing of the negotiations was excellent,” my mother says as she approaches.

“How come?” I ask.

“Now that they are out of the way, you can focus on your upcoming insemination.”

Oh, yes. The insemination will have no effect.

“Of course, mother. I will do as you wish.”

“Good,” she replies, sitting down next to me on the stone bench, as her guards step away to a respectful distance.

I try to peer into my mother’s mind, but I see darkness. She is hiding something from me. Even though our abilities grow stronger with each generation, mine being no exception, my mother has far more experience hiding her thoughts than I do.

She doesn’t know about my clandestine affair because I am naturally better, but I can not see her thoughts, as she has lived longer than I have.

I wonder what she is keeping from me.
