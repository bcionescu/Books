# Choices
### Adam

I look at the man in front of me. My eyes widen as I realize I’m looking at the Mayor. He is the last person I thought I’d see here. Has he come to drag me back to the Protectorate?

No, that doesn’t make sense. Why would he release Ambrosia and the Mater?

“Hold out your arm, boy,” the Mayor says.

Too shocked to speak, I raise my hand. The mayor grabs my wrist and places a device from his tool belt over it. I feel a warm stinging sensation before he lets go.

“You too,” he says, as he turns to the women.

“What is this for?” I ask, finally able to speak.

“I’m frying your chips,” he says. “We wouldn’t want the High Ones to be able to track you.”

“Do you need help with yours?” the Mater asks.

“I do not carry one,” he replies.

Of course.

“Now we need to get back,” says the Mayor, as he places the device back into his toolbelt.

“I assume we can’t go back the way you came?” asks the Mater.

“We could, but it would be risky. If anyone finds the bodies, the route will be swarming with guards.”

“There is a secret passageway that leads near an exit,” says the Mater.

“Who else knows about it?” asks the Mayor.

“My daughter and a few loyal guards.”

“Loyal to the Imperatrix now, I would imagine.”

“Likely, yes,” admits the Mater.

“Sir,” says Eve through the Mayor’s wristwatch.

“Yes, what is it?”

“The guards have found one of the bodies, and the Palace is now on high alert. Multiple guards are closing in on your position as we speak.”

“Which way to the secret passageway?”

“This way,” the Mater says, as she starts down the corridor. She stops in front of a tapestry that covers an entire wall.

“It’s behind here,” she says. The Mayor pulls a knife from his toolbelt and slashes the cloth, revealing a secret door.

The Mater reaches out and places her palm onto the reader. With a soft beep, the door slides open, and we make our way in.

Once we’re all inside the corridor, she turns around and places her hand on another reader. This time, the door slides closed.

“They’ll find the door soon enough, but they won’t be able to operate it,” the Mater says, as she looks up at the Mayor.

“Good,” he says, as he starts down the stairs. “Let’s go.”

Within a minute, we find ourselves at the bottom of the stairs, now on the ground floor.

The Mayor curses as he looks down at his wristwatch.

“What is the matter?” the Mater asks.

“There is someone right outside this door.”

“Could they have figured out where we went?” I ask.

“If that were the case, they would have sent more than one guard,” the Mayor replies. “Unless there are more on the way, and he just happened to be closest. Love?”

“Yes, dear,” the Mater responds, as she moves closer to the door.

“He has no idea that we’re in here,” she replies.

“How do you know that?” I ask.

“No time,” says the Mayor, as he searches through his toolbelt, pulling out a device. “Cover your ears.”

As we do so, he presses a button, which is followed by a large explosion upstairs.

“I figured this might come in handy,” he says, smiling, as he examines his wristwatch.

“Good,” the guard is moving away.

The Mater walks forward and presses her palm against the reader. The door opens with a soft whoosh, and the Mayor slices through the tapestry.

“The exit is right over there,” the Mater says.

“Eve,” the Mayor whispers into his wristwatch, “bring the vehicle closer to us.”

“I’ll be there in a few moments,” she responds.

The Mayor checks his wristwatch once more, ensuring that there is nobody else around.

“Eve is near. Let’s head out.”

As we step outside, we are met with an Atomizer pointed at us.

“Put your weapon down,” the guard says.

How did he not show up on the wristwatch scanner?

My eyes are drawn to his wrist, wrapped in a bloody bandage. He realized that we must have tracking technology, and so he cut out his own chip.

“Let’s talk about this,” says the Mayor.

“You are in violation of the law,” says the guard, as he pulls a knife out of his belt with his free hand. “Put your weapons down, this is your last warning.”

“I don’t want to hurt you,” says the Mayor. “It’s two against one,” he adds, as he tilts his head over at my raised Atomizer.

“I’ll take my chances,” the guard says.

“I know you’re scared,” says the Mater.

The guard’s eyes shoot over to her.

“You know you’re at a disadvantage. Even if you kill one of us, you’re also going to die. Walk away.”

“That is not going to—”

The guard screams in pain as he falls to the ground.

“Bloop bloop,” says GG, as he appears out of thin air, his shock weapon drawn.

“GG,” I cry, happy to see him.

“Beep beep bloop,” he chirps, dancing.

“Eve and the camouflaged ship must be right behind the guard,” the Mayor says, as he checks his wristwatch.

“That is correct,” Eve replies.

With a whoosh, all of our attention is redirected to the Mater, as she looks down at her abdomen, a knife now sticking out of it.

“No,” screams the Mayor, as his own knife swooshes through the air, becoming stuck in the guard’s throat.

“It is quite allright,” says the Mater, as she looks at the Mayor and me, her cheeks damp.

“There is a medical pod inside the vehicle,” the Mayor says, as he grabs the Mater and carries her inside. “Eve, activate the pod.”
