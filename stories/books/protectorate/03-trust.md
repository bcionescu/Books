# Trust
### Head Praetorian

Staring out into space, I feel lost in thought. My head turns as someone knocks on the door.

“Come in,” I say.

The door slides open, and a young man steps through.

“Praetorian A-376, sir, reporting as requested.”

Examining the young Praetorian’s facial features for a moment, I note just how much he looks like me when I was his age.

“Do you know why you are here?” I ask.

“No, sir.”

Is he really ready for this? Has Eve made a mistake? Did Damian really think this through?

“The mayor has informed me that he wishes to assign a mission to you. Before I send you off to him, I wanted to first give you a warning.”

The kid’s jaw clenches as he receives the news. Is he excited? Angry? Confused? Can he truly be trusted with this?

“I have known the mayor for a long time, since before you were created. He is a very diplomatic man, which makes him incredibly dangerous.”

Pausing for a moment before continuing, I add, “When he offers you this mission, he will phrase it in a way that might make you think you have a choice in the matter, as if it is optional. He might even ask you what you think about it. He is like that, you see.”

The boy says nothing, so I continue, “I, for one, am less diplomatic. I do not know whether you can truly be trusted with this. I hope you will prove me wrong, as I do see potential in you; however, know that once the mayor offers you the mission, you will be on your own. There will be nothing that either I or he can do to help you, and you will graciously accept.”

This kid’s quite tough. So far, he has only betrayed himself once. I wonder what he’s thinking right now. Is he trying to figure out what kind of reward he will receive? Is he angry? Does he feel helpless?

“Do you understand?”

“Yes, sir.”

A ray of light catches the kid’s green eyes behind his dark blonde hair as he bows.

“Dismissed.”

I turn to face my computer as the young Praetorian salutes and walks out.

With a few keystrokes, I open a private communication channel on my machine and begin typing a message.

### Adam

As I step outside the Head Praetorian’s office into the fresh air, I can feel my knees shaking. Why did the head Praetorian feel the need to warn me about this mission?

As I think, I make my way to the mayor’s office.

Would the mayor truly give me an option? If so, what would happen if I say no? Would I be informed that I have to do it anyway, or would the mayor apparently accept my decision, only to make my life a living hell afterwards? Does saying no count as treason? Will I be executed for refusing?

Whatever the case, I have no choice in the matter. I will speak to the mayor and learn the specifics of the mission.
