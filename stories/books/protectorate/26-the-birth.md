# The Birth
### Imperatrix, Twenty-One Years Earlier

Screams echo through the room as I grab onto the edges of the table.

“We’re nearly there,” says the geneticist. “I can see her head.”

Covered in sweat, I push one last time and feel my baby slide out, as I lay my head back down on the table in relief.

The newborn is crying and appears to be healthy, although the doctor and nurses are quiet.

I know why, of course.

I have been communicating with her, whilst she was still in my womb, for months. When she first reached out to me mentally, I was excited, realizing that my daughter had indeed inherited the genetic ability and appeared to be using it earlier than anyone before her.

However, my heart ached with pain upon the realization that the baby was not a she at all, but a he.

According to our highest laws, I am obligated to terminate the pregnancy, as I have to give birth to a girl. I had to, but having spoken to him already many times, the decision was not as easy for me as my Mater had told me.

Besides, this had never happened before. A male with sight? I was afraid of my Mater finding out, so I hid the gender of the baby from her, making sure that I always thought of him as a her, up until this very moment, when I am no longer able to protect him.

My head turns to examine my Mater’s face. Her expression is stony and unmoved.

Had she known this entire time?

Her eyes move onto mine, confirming that she had indeed known. Why did she not say anything? Was she also concerned about the development, about the first male with sight?

“Leave us,” says the Mater. “None of you is to ever speak of this,” she adds.

The geneticist places the baby in my arms, and they all exit the room, leaving the three of us alone.

“I’m sorry.”

“Stupid child,” the Mater says, sitting down on the edge of the table, caressing my forehead. There is sadness in her voice as she allows her grandson to suckle on her finger.

“I knew, of course. Your thoughts have always been enveloped in fog, as your ability is greater than mine; however, I knew. I suspected you were already pregnant the day of your insemination. I knew it was a boy, and that you had decided to keep him.”

The baby boy looks up at his grandmother and smiles as he continues to suckle on her finger.

“Then you also know how much cloudier his thoughts are.”

“Cloudy? I can’t make them out at all,” the Mater says. “This boy has a terrifying power. You may be able to make out some of his thoughts, but I can not. To him, I would one day be an open book, if only he had been allowed to live.”

I pull my baby close to my chest, saying, “He will live.”

“How?” asks my Mater, tears streaming down her face, searching my mind. “The father,” she continues.

“Yes, the father,” I echo.

“You can’t send this boy down there,” the Mater says. “They’ll cut him down in the prime of his life.”

“Better than dying now,” I say, looking down at him, caressing his cheek.

The Mater stands up, pacing around the room.

“I wish you had terminated the pregnancy when you were supposed to,” she says. “We wouldn’t be in this predicament if you had.”

“Would you have been able to?” I say, my eyes narrowing. “Search my mind, examine the connection between us. See our exchanges when he was still in my womb.”

“I already have,” says the Mater.

“Then you know.”

“Yes, I do,” she admits.

Silence falls upon the room as we look down at the baby.

Did you have to terminate a pregnancy? I think.

No, thinks the Mater. I don’t know if I would have been able to do it either, she confesses.

“It is settled, then,” says the Mater. “I will take him to a Source point and place him on the next shipment down to the Protectorate. Do you wish the father to know?”

“We’ll take him there together,” I say, slowly getting off the table. “And yes, I do. He deserves to know, and maybe that knowledge will save our boy’s life one day.”

We are escorted to a vehicle by two of our most trusted guards and driven to the appropriate Source. Upon arrival, I look up at the beautiful church, which now houses nightmares.

Upon entering, we find two dresses preparing three boys for shipment. They bow once they see us.

“Your Brightness,” the two dresses say in tandem.

I approach them and say, “You seem to have missed this one,” as I hand the boy over to them, my face puffy and red.

The dresses understand, as they place him in the transport pod, along with the other three boys.

“You are not to speak of this to anyone,” Mater says.

The dresses look over at her and bow once more, indicating that they understand.

I watch as the women load the pod into its shoot and type in the appropriate commands. The glass door of the pod closes down before it speeds away towards the Protectorate.

Upon return to the Palace, I walk over to the terminal in my chambers and sit down.

He should know, he has the right to, and maybe, just maybe, he can do something to protect our son in there.

He has your eyes, I finish typing, before pressing Send.
