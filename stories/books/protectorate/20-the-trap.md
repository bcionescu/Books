# The Trap 
### ???

As the sun begins to set, the light takes on a golden hue, bathing the glistening white towers of the Capitol in shimmering light.

The Imperatrix seems like a complicated woman. She has an unbelievable amount of power, but at the same time, she seems equally as trapped as the rest of us. She had inherited the throne, and now she has to pay for the many mistakes of those before her.

The Mater, on the other hand, is impenetrable. She has yet to speak a single word to us, thus making her impossible to read. I wonder what she looks like under that veil. If the High Ones live for a very long time, they must age at a slower rate, and thus, it’s doubtful that she looks that much different to the Imperatrix herself.

If she took off her veil, she’s likely to look more like her sister than her mother.

I turn around and begin to pace the room. Why am I thinking about the Mater? Why is this relevant? Strategy should be at the front of my mind, and yet, all I can think about is how the Imperatrix’s dress tightly fits her curves.

“Yes?” I answer, as a knock on the door wakes me from my reverie.

“Come with me,” the guard says, his hand over his weapon.

Am I about to die?

I straighten my shoulders and follow him out the door. If I am to die, I will do so on my feet.

As the guard leads me through the palace, the walls illuminate as they detect our presence.

“Right through here,” the guard says as he knocks on the door.

“Enter,” I hear someone say from the other side.

Was the Imperatrix going to kill me herself? Was she that sadistic? Did the stunt that we pulled earlier cross a line, and now we are to pay for it with our lives? If so, why only bring me? Why not both of us?

I take a deep breath and open the door, accepting my fate. As I step into the large room, I see the Imperatrix lying on a bed on the other end of the room.

“You can come closer.” Her voice echoes through the dimly lit chamber.

As I approach her, my eyes begin to adjust to the low light. The Imperatrix lies flat on the bed, her sheer dress revealing subtle details of her body underneath.

I swallow dryly.

“Do make yourself comfortable,” she says, as she taps the empty side of the bed next to her.

Unsure of what her intentions are, I sit at the edge of the bed and begin taking my shoes off. Until a few hours ago, I didn’t even know women existed.

First, she sends those dresses to our chambers, and now this. Whilst this is clearly a manipulation tactic, designed to give them the upper hand, I can’t help but feel excited.

Why did I react so strongly to seeing the outline of her body? I’ve seen the bare chests of my brothers in the Protectorate, and they never caused such a reaction in me. Why is it different with women?

As I take off my shoes, I can feel her sit up and scoot behind me, resting her head on my shoulder, and wrapping her arms around my neck. I try to ignore the feeling of euphoria as her chest presses against my back.

“Have you ever been with anyone?”

What does she mean?

“Of course, I’m sorry,” she says, her voice soft and yielding. “I forgot that you’ve never met women before.”

“That’s because you’ve killed them all,” I find myself blurting out. Damn it, I’m already starting to lose control.

I can’t let this woman get into my mind.

The Imperatrix exhales sharply through her nose, appearing to be slightly amused.

“I did no such thing,” she says.

Of course, I remember.

She’s trapped, just like the rest of us. The women of the Protectorate were killed centuries ago, before she and I were born. All that we can do is play our hand, and nothing more.

Even someone in her position is bound by the choices and mistakes of those who came before her.

“I understand. You are imprisoned, just like the rest of us.”

Her arms tense for a moment before allowing herself to lean even more into me.

“If I didn’t know any better, I’d think you could read my mind,” she whispers.

“I don’t need to read your mind to know as much,” I reply.

The Imperatrix kisses my shoulder, and I allow her to pull me down on my back, our feet now hanging off opposite sides of the bed.

An internal war has begun, where the rational side of my mind has to kill the primal side. I may not know much about women, but I can tell what is happening. If this is how men and women used to procreate, that genetic knowledge exists deep inside of me. Centuries of social programming can not remove that.

I understand now. Desiderium, a word from a long-forgotten language, which silently mocks every brother I have ever known. A pain so entrenched in our society that we have given it a name, a yearning for something we can not identify, a thirst which we can never quench.

“Why am I here?” I ask with a dry throat, as the Imperatrix presses her forehead against mine, breathing me in.

“Does there have to be a logical reason for why I would want you here?” she asks, her voice warm and forgiving.

“We are in the middle of negotiations, so I can’t help but question your motives,” I murmur.

“Diplomatic talks,” she corrects me, giggling.

“Right,” I say, unable to suppress a smile.

“Can I ask you something?” she asks, as she gently kisses my forehead.

“Go ahead.”

“Can we not talk about work right now?”

I hesitate for a moment before replying, “Ok.”

Without thinking, I raise my arm and run my hand through her long blonde hair.

“Am I the only one you have invited into your chambers?” I ask mindlessly.

“Are you asking if I’ve invited your friend over as well?”

“Yes.”

“Oh, I’m offended that you would even ask,” she says, pouting and feigning hurt.

I chuckle and turn my head to the side, examining her features. Her green eyes look back at me over her straight nose and generous lips.

“That was not my intention. I’m simply trying to figure out what is happening, that’s all. You do have me at a disadvantage.”

“Oh, how so?”

You know what I mean.

She smiles and shrugs playfully.

After a few moments of silence, she responds, “No, I did not invite your friend. Just you.”

I do not need the ability to read minds to know that she is telling the truth.

“So, what is the reason why I’m here?” I ask again, smiling.

“Oh, you men and your reasons,” she says.

“You’re an Imperatrix, you can’t afford to not have a reason for your actions,” I reply with a faint smile.

Her face becomes overwhelmed with sadness, and I find myself moving closer to her, wishing to console her somehow.

“One of the reasons why I like you is your intelligence. I can’t ignore its dangers, and yet, I can’t help but find myself helplessly attracted to you.”

“One of the reasons? Are there more?” I ask.

“Well, you’re not that bad looking, you know.”

“Thanks,” I chuckle. “You’re not too bad looking yourself, you know?”

The Imperatrix playfully punches me in the shoulder, as she begins to laugh.

I find that I like this version of her a lot more, unburdened by her political role.

“Would you mind terribly if I shared a burden with you?”

Interesting that she chose the word burden, moments after I thought of it.

“Of course,” I reply.

Of course? Why of course? Why did I yield so easily?

“In a few days, I am to be artificially inseminated. That is how our family reproduces.”

Interesting. So it is true that their abilities only pass along the female line. Because of that, they can not allow any man to have political power. They must use men purely for breeding and military purposes.

“That is true,” she says, cutting off her sentence abruptly.

Careful, empress, I think to myself as I smile. You’re slipping.

“I gather you do not wish to go through with the process?”

“Not particularly, no. I wish to have a daughter, but I do not wish to be inseminated. Such an ugly and clinical thing. I would like to have a child,” she hesitates for a moment, before finishing her thought, “the natural way.”

I fight the uncontrollable urge to press my face against hers. I don’t know to what purpose, but I instinctively know it would feel good.

“Until a few hours ago, I didn’t even know women existed,” I say. “I have no experience with such matters.”

“Would it surprise you to learn that I also share your lack of experience?”

The more I think about it, the more it makes sense. She lives in a highly controlled society, just as I do. Even though up here, men and women know about each other, that does not mean that she has any more freedom.

From the moment she was born, her entire life was planned for her, and since the lineage of the family is important, it makes sense that she would not be allowed to have any relations.

“What about the guard outside?” I ask.

“He is loyal to me. He will tell no one.”

This is an interesting proposition. She means for us to procreate. A child that would result from such an endeavor might prove to be a useful bargaining chip.

As I lay there, looking at her angelic face, I hate myself for having such a cynical thought.

“There’s that dangerous intelligence that I was referring to,” she says with a smile.

How deeply can this woman peer into my thoughts?

“You know, I am aware that you can read our minds at least two levels down,” I say, examining her pink lips.

“How so?” the lips inquire.

“We hatched a plan to figure out how much you can see. One of us was to imagine you exposed, as best we could, given how little we know, and neither of us was to think about the plan itself.”

She smiles at me, her eyes examining every feature of my face.

“And what makes you think I could see any of that?”

“Your reaction was careless. We figured that if one of us does that, you’ll react instinctively, and look at him, only for your eyes to then dart at the other, the one who had the idea.”

“I suppose my reaction was rather careless, but maybe I liked it.”

“That eventuality we did not consider,” I admit, as I inch my head closer to hers.

“So, what is your answer?”

“Yes, I would like to be with you, but I do not know how.”

“Let’s find out together,” she says, as her mouth finds mine.
