# Family
### Samantha

I sit on the floor, my back against the throne, face buried in my hands. Across from me sits the Mater, my mother, chained, flanked by two guards.

“Leave us.”

The two guards bow, turn around, and exit the room, leaving the two of us alone.

“Why have you done this?”

Silence.

I look up at her, and I see that she is crying as well.

“Why have you done this?” I ask again, my voice louder.

The question might sound vague to anyone else, but not to her. My mother would know exactly what I mean because she can peer into my mind, the one thing I can not do, and she knows this.

“Why have you done this to me?” I ask for a third time. “Why have you put me in this position?”

“I’m sorry,” the Mater whispers, her cheeks damp.

“For what?” I ask in a hoarse voice, burying my face back in my hands.

“For everything. For the fact that you do not have the sight, for the fact that you still have to go on as Imperatrix despite this, for helping him escape, for everything.”

My mother’s voice is quiet; quiet enough that it does not reverberate against the walls of the throne room. Quiet enough for me to understand that these words are meant for me, and me alone.

“Why did you help him?” I ask, exhausted.

“I’m sorry,” she responds.

“It’s him, isn’t it?” I ask.

She remains quiet, and so I continue. “He is your son, my brother.”

I look up to see that her eyes are fixated upon my shoes, tears pouring down her cheeks. I know this is true; I do not need to be able to peer into her mind to know this.

“What were you doing on the terminal? When you were arrested, the guards told me you were using it.”

“He had a right to know.”

“Who? Who had a right to know?”

“The father,” she whispers.

“Who is the father? What is his name?” I find myself screaming at her.

I take a moment to breathe.

“We never have enough choices, never enough, mostly none, not even in our position. When a choice presents itself, you take it. You take it and run, and hope nobody ever tries to grab it from you.”

“Is that what happened?” I ask. “You made a choice?”

“Yes,” the Mater answers. “I had a son, and I gave him away. One was a choice, the other was not.”

“You would have kept him?” I asked.

“Yes. I would have kept him and raised him, but that was not an option. We never have choices.”

Her shoulders begin to tremor as she starts to cry harder.

All I can do is sit in silence, watching the woman who has cursed me cry helplessly.

“You will both die tomorrow.”

She nods, understanding.

Her calm acceptance is too much, and I feel tears begin to flow down my own cheeks once more. Tomorrow, I will have to put my own mother to death. I have no choice; the betrayal has been too public.

I find myself thinking about choices and how we never have enough of them.

“Guards,” I shout.

Within moments, the two guards reappear.

“Take her back to the dungeon,” I order, as I stand up and sit on my throne.

“Yes, your Brightness,” they say, as they carefully pick up the Mater and escort her back to her cell.

She is right, of course. She always is. We never have enough choices. We think we do, and then we find ourselves in a trap, unable to move.

There is, however, one choice I can make. Do I wish to meet him before he dies tomorrow?

Why? Why make it harder on myself? I can’t spare him, either of them. I have no choice. I will attend the execution, but I must not speak with him. It will be easier this way.

As I begin to cry once more, my whimpers echo off the cold, tall walls of the throne room, dappled sunlight dancing, mocking me as my tears flow.
