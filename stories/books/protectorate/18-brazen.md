# Brazen
### Octavian

Good, everything is going according to plan. We have delivered the accusation, and since we are not dead, this indicates that the High Ones want something from us.

Now, it is my turn.

As my eyes move to the Imperatrix, my gaze focuses on her body, and then her chest. Whilst I have never seen a woman bare, she can’t look that different from a brother.

I need to do my best and imagine her exposed, in front of me, in the most compromising of positions. As my eyes dart up to her face, I can see surprise, her eyes narrowing. Next, her eyes move over to Damian.

Perfect.

It worked.

Damian delivered the accusation, so there is no reason for her eyes to fixate on me. She could see my thoughts.

Since her eyes then darted to him, she is aware that he was the one to suggest the plan; therefore, she can likely peer at least two levels deep into our minds.

“My apologies, your Brightness, we do not mean to accuse you personally of this atrocity, only that someone in your position, a long time ago, is responsible,“ says Damian. “We thank you for the invitation, and we are happy to reinitiate diplomatic relationships to repair the wrongdoings of the past.”

Damian has now delivered the full message. How will they react?

“While we do not acknowledge any wrongdoing on our part, it is always good to maintain diplomatic relations with neighboring states, and thus, we welcome dialogue.”

“That being said,“ the Imperatrix continues, “I think we have made enough progress for today. We would like to pause discussions for now and continue tomorrow.”

“You are very wise,” I reply, as my eyes move to the clasped hands of the Mater. Whilst she has been quiet the entire time, it is clear that the two have been communicating silently.

What does the Mater think of all of this, of the brazen accusations, and the mental images of her daughter? Are they so desperate that they would do nothing about it? Perhaps they are, in fact, incapable of reading our minds, or maybe they are not as capable as we had imagined.

Within moments, the guard appears behind us and takes us back to our room.

“So, Eve, how did we do?” I ask.

“Splendid,” she replies. “We have now confirmed that the High Ones want something from us, and they are desperate enough to gloss over such accusations. The next step is to figure out exactly what they do want. They will likely offer us technology in an attempt to appear magnanimous, or they will attempt to learn what advancements we have made. We could then offer something in exchange, and see how excited they appear by the prospect.”

“What should we offer them first?” I ask.

“Our sub-dermal tracking technology would be a good start,” suggests Eve. “It has no military application, at least not one that can be used against us.”

“And it looks like the Imperatrix was able to see two levels down,” says Damian.

“Yes,” I add. “When I pictured her exposed in front of me, it seemed like she reacted, and then she looked over at you, indicating that she knew you were responsible.”

“That is true,” Damian says. “However, it’s impossible to know with complete certainty. It is possible that she looked at both of us at the wrong time, unable to believe we would levy such accusations against them.”

“We’ll have to wait and see,” I conclude.
