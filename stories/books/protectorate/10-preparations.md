# Preparations
### Adam

I curse, walking away from the table.

“What’s wrong?” asks V, grinning. “Afraid a little bit of rope,” he continues, as he takes the climbing gear out of the case.

I feel sick, my body trembling.

Escaping was never going to be easy. I had imagined running from Praetorians, being shot at, even having to kill to get out, but this?

“I know this is not ideal, kid,” but we have no choice, Sylvan says as he takes his gear out.

“So we’re climbing up to the vents?” I ask, my voice shaking.

“The Protectorate is built inside a massive cave system. This will allow us to climb up to the vents and squeeze through, making our way to the ventilation service tunnels,” says Octavian.

“As I was searching through the data, I found old blueprints of the Protectorate. They show a locked door in the ventilation systems. Behind it is a very, very long tunnel,” says V, as he equips his gear.

“A way out?” I ask.

“It has to be,” says V. “It’s the only thing that looks remotely like a way out.”

“So it’s possible that it may not be a way out. This can all be for nothing?” I ask.

“Sure,” says V. “We won’t know until we try, though.”

“Why don’t we sneak into the ventilation system through one of the service entrances?”

“Too risky,” replies Octavian. “It’s dangerous as it is, as we may be spotted climbing up, or we might run into maintenance workers.”

“If we do run into anyone,” V says, “we can stun them. That’ll solve the problem, assuming we don’t trigger some sort of alarm.”

“Grab your gear,” says Octavian. “It’s dark out, and the synth-moon is in its new moon phase—”

“This means,” interrupted V, “that tonight is as dark as it can be.”

“This is our best chance,” says Octavian.

I walk over to the table and begin equipping my gear.

“Everyone ready?” asks Octavian. “Atomizers set to stun?”

We check our weapons and nod. Octavian walks up to each man and ensures their gear is properly equipped.

He then opens his backpack, checking its contents. I remember that this is the same one Augustus had when I first met him. It must be full of explosives.

“Just in case,” says V, winking at me.

“V,” starts Octavian, “is the data set up for upload?”

“Yes,” V replies. “There’s a lot of it, so it will take some time. It should be all publicly available by the time we’re out.”

“Alright then, let’s move out.”

My ears ring as a massive explosion sends everything into chaos.
