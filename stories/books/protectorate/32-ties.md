# Ties
### ???

I press a combination of buttons on my wristwatch, narrowing its scanning area.

Turning my attention back to the vehicle, I push down on the controller and begin to slide forward. Turning my head to the left, I look out the window to see a soldier smoking, looking right through me.

I continue moving further, careful not to increase my speed, lest my camouflage begin to diminish. As long as I move slowly, the craft will be invisible and inaudible, allowing me to sneak my way past the guards.

Am I too late?

Expertly manipulating the controls, I slowly make my way to the Palace. Having arrived at one of the service entrances, I get out of my chair, and by the dim light of the electronic displays, I open the weapons chest.

This finally comes in handy.

Reaching into the chest, I pull out an Atomizer, checking to make sure it is set to Vaporize. Unfortunately, nobody can know I was here.

My eyes wander to the grenades, and I pick up a few, placing them into my belt pouch.

“Eve,” I say, “can you confirm that the security system is disabled?”

“Only partially,” says a soft female voice emanating from the display panel on the vehicle. “I am currently manipulating the video feeds so that they loop seamlessly; however, the alarms have been modified since you were here last. If they are triggered, I would not be able to stop them.”

“If they sound a silent alarm, would you be able to notify me?”

“Yes,” Eve says.

“Have you been able to access their logs? How much time do I have?” I ask.

“They are to be escorted to the execution site at sunrise,” she says in a calm voice.

So I have less than an hour.

I take my backpack off one more time, open it, and check that the explosives are still in there. If I have to use these, it means things have gone wrong.

I’m as ready as I’ll ever be. All that is left to do is to hit the button next to me, and the door will open.

“Wish me luck?” I ask.

“Good luck,” the voice says in a sultry tone.

I check my wristwatch once more; no human lifeforms in the vicinity. I program the watch to notify me if anyone does come close, and, exhaling, I press the button; the door slides open without a sound.

Jumping down on the grass, I am overcome with nostalgia.

I never realized how much I missed this place.

Even though my watch will warn me if humans are near, it will not work for those mutant mutts; I have to be careful not to rouse them.

I blink twice, activating the night vision capability of my ocular implant. It is an older model, so it is not able to highlight moving objects, but it is better than nothing.

I curse, admonishing myself for never having the implant upgraded over the years.

Walking up to the entrance, I press my ear to it; nothing, it is safe to go through. I place an electronic lock pick near the palm reader, and the door slides open, allowing me to sneak through.

Good thing they never really understood the security system.

Once inside, I check the map on my wrist, making my way down the corridor. My wristwatch buzzes quietly, warning me of my proximity to another human. Looking down at it, I see that he is walking towards me from the room to the left, potentially opening the door at any moment.

I look down, considering whether I should use the Atomizer. It works very well, but it also makes a mess, and I do not want to attract any attention. As the door slides open, the guard walks through.

His scream is muffled by my hand, as a sharp prick in his neck leaves him lifeless.

I retract the poisoned needle back into its handle and place the weapon back into my utility belt. Double-checking my watch, I make sure nobody else is around and drag the body of the guard back into the room, hiding it as best I can.

Hoping the body won’t be discovered until I’m gone, I walk back into the hallway and continue moving north.

As I go up to the second floor, crawling on my hands and knees up the stairs, I spot one of the mutant mutts, sleeping in front of the door I need to walk through.

My suit covers my scent, which is only useful as long as the mutt does not hear or see me. Checking my wristwatch, I see two lifeforms in the room behind the mutt.

I can’t use the Atomizer; it would be too loud, alerting the men. I have to take the mutt out and do it fast. I pull an electro-grenade from my utility pouch and throw it at the creature. As it lands with a muffled clang, the mutt immediately stands up, only to be cut down by the electric field produced by the weapon.

I exhale slowly, relieved that the mutt did not make much sound. As I stand up, my watch buzzes, causing me to drop back down immediately. The door next to the mutt opens, and two guards step outside, laughing.

“And then I say—” the man pauses, still grinning, looking down at the creature.

“Fluffy?”

Gargles follow the name of the unfortunate soul on the floor, as the guard drowns in his blood; the knife swooshes through the air, catching him by surprise.

The other guard is petrified and confused. The last thing he sees is my eyes, as I squeeze the life out of him. He dies moments later, confused, with many questions unanswered.

I sit for a moment, catching my breath. I look down at my most recent victims, a mutated human called Fluffy and two guards, both guilty and innocent at the same time. Throughout their lives, they have likely committed countless crimes; however, neither of them ever really had a choice.

Nobody does, I suppose.

I drag the three lifeless bodies back into the room.

With every kill I make, I shorten my available time. The more guards go missing, the sooner someone will notice and trigger an alarm.

Eve is looking out for me, jamming the cameras, but eventually, someone is going to notice something amiss.

Resigned to this fact, I check my watch, pull my knife out of the first guard’s throat, and walk back into the corridor, closing the door behind me.

One more floor, and I’ll be there.

Climbing the next set of stairs, I see a young man guarding a door. Unfortunately for him, this is a door that I have to pass through. As the guard moves, his features are illuminated, and I hesitate. He looks young, much too young.

Damn it.

The young guard looks a lot like my son. I will not be able to kill him. Doing so would make things much easier, but I can not bring myself to do it. I look at my Atomizer and reluctantly set it from vaporize to stun.

Checking my watch, I ensure that there are no other guards nearby. I lay down on the stairs, thinking. If I stun, I’ll have to run to him and make sure he does not hit his head. However, just because the watch shows no human life signs, that doesn’t mean that there are no mutts around. Stunning the young man will create some noise, and that’s the last thing I want.

Sweating, I aim the Atomizer at the guard and fire. The young man’s muscles seize all at once, paralyzing him for a short time. I run up the remaining stairs and jump, catching him before his head hits the stone floor.

I immediately regret my decision. My gaze darts to the left, where I meet the red eyes of a mutant mutt, its teeth sunk into my forearm. It takes everything I have not to scream as the creature sinks its fangs deeper into my flesh.

Panicking, I reach for my Atomizer. I can not risk using stun. As the mutt’s teeth are too deep into my body, the electric signal might conduct through the creature and reach me too, knocking me out in the process.

My right hand shaking, I manage to switch the weapon to vaporize, aim at the mutant, and pull the trigger. As the radiation from the weapon reaches the body of the mutt, it picks up its genetic signature and targets every one of its cells, turning the creature into a fine mist.

As the Atomizer is programmed to ignore my genetic makeup, I am not vaporized, although the wound on my forearm remains, and my eyes now need to be washed of blood.

Reaching for a small bottle in my pouch, I pour some of its contents into my eyes and wince as I regain my sight. Pouring the rest of the bottle onto my wounded forearm, I pull out a small kit from my belt and open it. My hands still shaking with adrenaline, I pull out an ointment, which I apply to the wound, trying not to scream in pain as I do so.

Having wrapped my forearm in a black bandage, I put the kit back in my belt and tie up the young guard.

Checking to make sure the bandage is solid, I reach into a different pouch and take out a small bottle. As I unscrew its cap, its stench reaches my nostrils, and I struggle not to gag.

Pouring the solution all over the floor, I remind myself that it will dissolve any blood that I might have spilled, making it impossible for the High Ones to trace my identity.

“When you wake up, try to stay unconscious for a while longer, will you?” I whisper to the unconscious body as I walk away.

I know I am getting close. My watch indicates four human life signs in the room up ahead. Edging closer to it, I can hear the guards, the door slightly ajar.

“You should have heard him,” one of them says. “Noo, noo, please don’t,” he screams in a mocking tone. The other guards laugh heartily.

As the laughter subsides, one of them asks, “Grunwald, what do you think will happen with the Mater?”

“I don’t know. The Imperatrix is in charge, and we’ll do as we’re ordered,” he replies.

“What about the girl?”

“Well, who’s to say we can’t have a bit of fun with her before we end her, ey?” They laugh even harder as my stomach turns in disgust.

“She knocked me out the other day, she did, before escaping with the lad. She won’t just get away with it.”

“And the lad? The one from the Protectorate?” a new voice asks.

“Well, technically, we have to return him.”

“Is it just him left?”

“Yeah,” replies Grunwald, “the other two are dead.”

“If it were up to me,” he continues, “I would have killed them when they last tried to escape. That bloody git killed my friend!” he spits, continuing, “Anyway, the Imperatrix, her Brightness, has decided that all three of them are to die. If the Protectorate asks any questions, we’ll deny any knowledge.”

I have heard all that I need to. Reaching into my belt, I grab a small gas grenade, activate it, and slowly roll it into the room, pulling up the mask built into my suit.

Within moments, the chatter stops, as chairs scrape, and bodies thump to the floor. I go in to make sure everyone is dead.

All the bodies are still, but as I check my watch, I notice one life sign remains. Moving over to it, I roll the man over with my boot.

“Please, sir, spare me,” the man says. I recognize him as the one who mocked his dying victim and suggested that they might harm the young woman.

The knife flies out of my hand and sticks into his throat. A few gurgles later, the watch shows no life signs remaining in the room.

So far, I have killed seven guards, stunned one, and also gotten rid of two mutts. I know the noose is tightening, as the odds of leaving undiscovered are now close to zero.

I search their pockets and find what I am looking for: an ID card, speckled with blood, but otherwise functional. I also relieve them of their gold rings, as they won’t need them anymore.

Taking off my backpack, I place it on the table, knocking over playing cards. Reaching my hands into it, I arm the explosives and then hide the bag underneath one of the bodies.

Just in case.

I step outside the room and walk over to the dungeon door. Checking my wristwatch, I note that all of the life forms in the dungeon are safely locked behind force fields, so there are no more guards to deal with.

I slide the door to the dungeon open by scanning the ID card and walk in. It is late at night, so most of the prisoners are asleep.

Checking my watch, I determine that my first target is in the next cell to the right. Walking noiselessly, I place one of the rings on my finger and walk right through the forcefield.

I wander over to the bed and look at her as she sleeps. Her blonde hair covers much of her face, red from crying.

She is beautiful.

My heart sinks as I recall the intentions of the guards.

I reach over to her face and put my hand over her mouth, her cheeks still moist. Immediately waking up, Ambrosia’s scream is stifled by my glove, as she tries to get away.

I lift my other finger to my mouth, indicating that she should be quiet. “I’m not here to hurt you,” I whisper, as her body relaxes.

“Can I trust you to be quiet?” I inquire.

She nods her head as I remove my hand from her face.

“Who are you?” she whispers.

“A friend.”

“Adam! We need to rescue him as well.”

“Be patient. Follow me, and try to make as little noise as possible,” I say, as I give her the second ring.

She follows me into the main corridor and carefully walks behind as I follow the map on my watch. Walking through the next forcefield, I step into the cell, only to be pushed by the Mater, desperately fighting for her life.

As our eyes meet, she freezes.

“You,” she murmurs, her voice shaking. Tears flood her eyes as her mouth finds mine. Ambrosia stands behind us, confused.

“What’s going on?” Ambrosia asks.

“There isn’t any time,” I say, coming up for air. “My love, we must hurry. We can catch up later,” I say, as I hand her the third ring.

The two women walk behind me, doing their best not to make a sound. Finding the last cell, we step inside.

Ambrosia rushes past me and hugs Adam, burying his face in her hair.

“What’s happening?” he asks, drunk with sleep.

“We’re being saved, I think,” Ambrosia whispers, as she moves aside to reveal the man.

“Hello, boy,” I say, looking at him.
