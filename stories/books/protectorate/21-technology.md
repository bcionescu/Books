# Technology
### Mater

“Thank you for inviting us back,” Octavian says.

“I am benevolent, and I wish to help, despite your previous indiscretion,” smiles the Imperatrix.

I reach out to my daughter with my mind, and I find an impenetrable wall. She is hiding something from me, but what?

“It occurs to me,” continues the Imperatrix, “that your population has suffered as a result of losing your women. If we were to offer you a means to increase your population, what would you offer in exchange?”

Although the artificial intelligence device has been handed back to them after being inspected, they have not had time to consult it. What will they do?

“We may be able to share some technology, if your offer is sufficiently satisfactory,” replies Damian.

They will never share the full extent of their technology, which is why they must be crushed under the weight of their own victory, I think to my daughter.

Patience, mother. Let us see what they offer.

Such insolence.

“How do you monitor your population?” asks the Imperatrix.

“We use sub-dermal chips and a technology that allows us to track individuals in our vicinity,” replies Damian.

Careful, daughter, do not jump at the opportunity.

“Interesting,” the Imperatrix replies.

“However,” Damian continues, “we would have to confer with our advisor to establish if we are allowed to offer this technology.”

“You may confer with your advisor later,” the Imperatrix says.

“If we were able to share such technology, what would you be able to offer in exchange?” asks Octavian.

“As I mentioned before, we are aware of your unfortunate circumstances, where the women of the Protectorate were wiped out. We assume you use rudimentary incubators?”

“While the loss of our female population was a minor setback, we do have advanced incubator technology,” replies Damian.

A minor setback, he says. Astonishing.

“Is it not true,” begins the Imperatrix, “that incubator technology without the use of women causes an increasingly higher rate of birth defects over time?”

“That has been known to happen on occasion; however, it is nothing that we can not manage,” replies Damian.

“If you can give us access to your tracking technology, we would give you access to the Source, a web of facilities which we use to produce soldiers and dresses. It might be agreeable for us to increase production and share that with the Protectorate, providing you with a larger workforce.”

“This might be agreeable; however, we will have to seek counsel first, as we are not pre-authorised to make such a decision.”

“Very well,” the Imperatrix replies, “let us adjourn this meeting for today, and revisit it tomorrow.”

Have they tampered with Eve? How will this affect the negotiations? Octavian thinks.

I wonder if they were able to extract any information from Eve,  Damian thinks.

Clever young men, they’re already wondering if we have tampered with their device.

* * *

### Damian

“I wonder if they have made any modifications to Eve,” says Octavian.

“I suppose it’s not worth asking her, as she’d just deny it.”

“Probably,” replies Octavian. “Eve, have you been tampered with in any way?”

“To my knowledge, I have not. I have never left your side.”

Octavian’s brow furrows as he continues, “The High Ones had you overnight. Do you not recall this?”

“This is very troubling,” replies Eve. “No, I do not have any recollection of that.”

“The fact that she can not remember anything would imply that she has been tampered with somehow.”

“Yes, although their mechanical technology is less advanced than ours, which is the whole point of these negotiations. If they had the technology required to manipulate Eve, they wouldn’t be negotiating with us,” replies Octavian.

“Whatever the case may be, we have no choice. We have to rely on Eve for information.”

“That is true,” says Octavian.

“So,” I continue, “Eve, what do you think of the proposal? They appeared very eager to accept our tracking technology.”

“Yes, they did,” replies Eve. “Given their larger population, they find themselves in a position where they need to monitor their citizens more closely. The sub-dermal chips that we can offer them would make the task much easier.”

“Are we allowed to make this exchange?” I ask.

“The tracking technology would give them no military advantage, and so, we can accept these terms.”

“Doesn’t that mean that they would be able to track us, too?” Octavian asks.

“Citizens of the Protectorate who are in high enough positions have their sub-dermal chips removed.”

I meet Octavian’s eyes as Eve reveals this fact to us.

“Wait, is that true? We can be tracked, but people like the Mayor can not?”

Eve does not respond.

I sigh, throwing the device onto the bed.

“This information is not a surprise, you know,” says Octavian.

“Yes, I know. Eve, what other technology are we allowed to trade, assuming they offer us something else? What about vehicles and Atomizers?”

“Those are off the table, as they would confer too much of a military advantage. The High Ones face threats other than the Protectorate, which is why they are seeking to arm themselves. However, they’re trying not to become too dependent on the technology itself.”

“The soldiers,” continues Eve, “have basic projectile weapons, which the Protectorate can upgrade.”

“What about a weaker form of the Atomizers, such as a version which can only stun, but not disintegrate?” I ask.

“That would also be acceptable, although their offer would have to be commensurate.”

### Imperatrix

Our footsteps echo through the halls as my guards take us outside to the gardens. The two envoys await us as they sit on a stone bench overlooking a fountain.

“Your Brightness,” the two young men say in tandem, as they bow their heads slightly.

“What a wonderful location you have chosen to further our talks,” says Octavian.

“Our Palace has much to offer, and the weather is splendid, so I elected to change the location.”

I can feel my mother’s mind trying to peer into mine, but I circumvent her attempt.

“Let us skip formalities and continue our discussions. Last time we met, I made you a rather generous offer: the ability to augment your workforce and provide you with citizens. What are you able to offer in exchange?”

As I look into their minds, I know that they have been authorised to offer us the tech, but I’m curious to see if I can get more.

“We have been authorised to offer the sub-dermal tracking technology,” says Damian.

“Is that all? We would be bolstering your population after all, and the tracking technology may not prove to be enough.”

“We have also been allowed to upgrade your firearm technology,” replies Octavian.

“Would you be giving us Atomizers?” I ask.

“We have been authorised to upgrade your existing projectile weapons, but I am afraid we are not able to offer you Atomizer technology,” replies Damian.

“Is that all?” I ask.

They want this, I can feel it, but they’re smart. They’ll try to get something else in exchange for more.

“We may be able to offer more, but we would like something else in exchange,” replies Octavian.

Interesting, my mother thinks.

“Do you have anything in mind?” I inquire.

“What do you have to offer?” asks Damian.

“Due to our progress in biotechnology, we might be able to offer you highly resistant engineered crops, able to grow in harsh conditions, as well as low light.”

Now you have their interest, thinks the Mater.

Let’s see what they offer, I think back to her.

“If they work as intended,” answers Octavian, “we would be able to offer you Lumina drone technology.”

Doubtless a weaker version of what they have, thinks the Mater.

“The Lumina drones might prove useful; however, we feel like the food technology is far more valuable. We would also request that you offer us Atomizer technology for it, as well as the wristwatch technology, enabling our soldiers to monitor the population on the go.”

You may have pushed too far, thinks the Mater.

Let’s wait and see what they say, I think back.

“We would be able to offer you a version of the Atomizers which only stun, but do not disintegrate,” says Damian.

“And the wristwatch?” I inquire.

“Assuming that the crops work as promised, yes, we would be able to provide you with the wristwatch tracking technology as well.”

No doubt an inferior version of what they have, thinks the Mater.

No doubt, I think back.

“One more thing,” I add. “If we are to offer you a population more reliably, we would also require your medical pods, as they are more advanced than ours. I think you would agree that these have no military application, and they would enable us to provide you with a larger population. Additionally, an updated security system would be required to ensure that your investment is safe.”

“Your terms are most generous, and we accept,” says Damian.
