# The Escape
### Adam

Struggling to breathe, I look through the rubble and smoke as someone shouts orders through the chaos.

I stand up, dazed but relatively unharmed.

“This way,” cries Octavian, as he pulls on a rug.

What am I doing? Where am I?

Moving the rug out of place, Octavian reveals a small hatch in the floor. V opens it, and we slide inside, making sure to cover it up with the carpet as we go down the ladder.

“It always pays to be paranoid,” laughs V, still shaking.

“What do we do now?” I ask.

Octavian unzips his backpack. “We proceed with the plan. This tunnel leads to multiple other service tunnels.”

He takes out some explosives and straps them to the wall, near the ladder.

“This should buy us some time,” he says, as he sets the timer for thirty seconds.

“Alright, let’s move out.”

We run down the tunnel and make it past the first blast door before the explosion happens.

V pulls out a device from his belt. “This way.”

“How far are we from a point where we can begin to climb?” asks Octavian.

“Not too far,” V replies. “The ideal point is quite far off, and now that we can’t get to the vehicle, we can’t reach it, not before morning light. We’ll have to climb to a closer vent, but this will leave us more exposed.”

“We’ll have to risk it,” says Octavian.

V leads us down what seems like a maze of underground service tunnels until we make our way outside. As the hatch opens, I realize we’re in a park. It’s still rather dark out, so we have a chance.

“The trees will hide us from prying eyes,” says V.

“Until we begin to climb,” adds Sylvan.

“It took too long to get here,” says Octavian, as I approach the stone wall. “The synth-Sun will begin to rise soon, so we need to go. Now!”

I make the mistake of looking up. The vents are near the cave ceiling, above the synth-sky projection. I estimate this to be about sixty feet up in the air.

I curse, wishing I hadn’t done that. Hands shaking, I begin climbing with the other men.

“If we keep this pace,” says V, “we’ll make it into the vent before the sun rises.”

The minutes feel like hours as we slowly inch up the stone wall.

“Careful,” cries Sylvan, as rocks fall onto him.

“Sorry,” says V.

“We need to keep moving,” cries Octavian. “We’re nearing the vents.”

Until this point, I had tried to look only in front of me. As I move my head downwards, I realize I have made a terrible mistake. My head spinning, I grip the side of the wall, cursing my decision.

“Don’t look down,” laughs Sylvan. “Come on, we’re halfway through.”

A few inches at a time, we continue to climb until we are a few feet away from the vents. My ears perk up, as my stomach sinks.

No, not now.

The familiar sound makes me feel sick. “Lumina drone,” I cry.

The other men’s heads shoot left and right, looking for it.

“Where?” hisses Octavian.

“I don’t know, but I can hear it. It’s close,” I say.

“Ok, we need to get moving, now!”

As we climb faster, we soon reach the point where the sky is projected. I hope this helps obscure us from the Lumina, but I know it likely does not.

Octavian reaches the vent first and climbs in, barely able to squeeze through. He is followed by V and Sylvan, and finally by me.

As the vent cover shuts with a clang, I can hear the buzzing sound of the drone on the other side.

We made it with no time to spare.

Squeezing my way through the vent, I turn around and follow the other men as we struggle to breathe in the confined space.

Mercifully, the other end of the vent opens into a service tunnel, and we get back on our feet.

“We made it,” laughs V.

“Quiet,” whispers Sylvan, although he too can barely hold his excitement.

“We’re not out of the woods yet,” confirms Octavian. “How far to the exit door?”

V takes out his device again and consults the map.

“Can you see workers on that thing?” asks Sylvan.

“No, this is not Praetorian-issue. I can work miracles, but I have not been able to get access to the lifeform tracker tech. We’ll just have to be careful.”

“This way,” he continues, pointing with his hand. We follow, as we listen for any signs of workers.

Nearing a junction, we hug the left wall as a voice rings down the corridor.

“My shift’s over, so I’m going to go have a few drinks. You coming?” says the man.

“Sure,” replies a second voice.

The maintenance tunnels are dark, so I squeeze myself as tightly as I can against the wall and hold my breath. With a bit of luck, the men will pass without noticing us.

As the voices approach from the left, I see Octavian squeezing his Atomizer, ready to stun them.

“Boy, this rat smells bad,” says the second man.

“It’s part of the job,” says the first. “You’ll get used to it.”

“I hope so,” says the second, laughing, as they take a left, now walking away from us.

We begin to breathe easier, as V indicates that we should go right.

Minutes go by as we navigate the maze of tunnels. Suddenly, a door opens on the left. A worker now stares at us.

“You guys shouldn’t be here,” he says, moments before collapsing to the floor.

Octavian opens the door the rest of the way, nudging it with his weapon, to see a scared young man shaking in the room. His hand is on the emergency button.

He curses, walking out. “The alarm has been triggered. How far?” asks Octavian.

“Not far,” says V. “We might as well run for it now.”

Sprinting, we make our way to the end of the corridor, where we find a door, unceremoniously placed right next to a janitor’s closet.

“No pad?” asks Sylvan.

“This door is as old as the bunker,” says V, as he looks through his tool belt. “An old-school door has an old-school lock.”

Finding his lockpicks, he crouches down, the Atomizer ray missing him by inches. I throw myself down on the ground, covering my head.

“Get it open,” cries Octavian, as he begins shooting back at the Praetorians. Sylvan and I join in, trying to buy V as much time as possible.

With a click, the door opens, and we rush inside, slamming it behind us.

In front of us is a very long corridor, with a platform and a small train. I try to see if I can spot the other end, but I can not, as the tunnel curves upwards.

“We need to move,” says Octavian, running onto the platform. Detecting our presence, the train opens its doors, allowing us in.

As I rush inside, the last one in, the doors close behind me, and the train starts to move slowly.

“Presumably, it has only one other stop?” asks Sylvan.

“I think so,” replies V. “The schematics only show two platforms, one here and one at the end.”

“And past the platform?” I ask.

“A large door,” says V.

That is our goal. Whatever happens, we have to get to it. Even if the Praetorians make it through the locked old-school door, we are on the only train.

“Did anyone else find this a bit too easy?” I ask.

“Easy? We nearly got arrested twice,” moaned V.

As the train approaches the end of the line, I can make out a figure on the platform. A man is standing by a large hatch, casually leaning onto the wall, Atomizer in hand.

“There’s someone there,” I say.

The men rush to the window.

Sylvan curses.

There is nothing we can do. We have no control over the train; we can not go back. Even if we could, where would we go? What would we do? We’d be hunted down one by one.

As the train comes to a stop with a loud hiss, the doors open, and we step onto the platform, Atomizers in hand.

“You finally showed up,” the mayor says, smiling.

“Octavian,” he says, nodding his head in his direction.

“Damian,” Octavian replies, eyes fixed on him.

“Hello, boy,” Damian says to me.

Unsure of what to do, I bow my head slightly, saying nothing.

“I see your mission went rather well,” he continues, “well, except for the deaths, but we don’t have to worry about those,” he says.

“You won’t be able to stop us,” says Sylvan, pointing his weapon at Damian. “There are four of us, and one of you.”

I look around. Why are there no Praetorians? Why did the mayor choose to meet us alone? Is this a trap? How did he get here ahead of us?

“Stop you?” says Damian. “I have no intention of doing such a thing.” He smiles, looking at our confused faces.

“You see, all of you have rather become a nuisance. You know too much, and attempts at finding you have failed. Now I know why,” he says, looking at Octavian and V.

“No, I shan’t stop you. In fact, I can not allow you to stay. You _have_ to leave,” he says, as he walks over to a computer terminal and begins typing.

“Your upload, by the way,” says the mayor, “I’m afraid it did not go through.” He looks up at V, mockingly pouting his lips at him.

V stares at him, but says nothing. We did not manage to shake the Protectorate to its core, but we might get away with our lives.

The large hatch behind the mayor begins to groan as the metal moves and turns, creating an opening in the wall.

“Off you go,” Damian says, smiling.

This has to be a trap. Are there Praetorians outside, ready to arrest us, or kill us? Why go through all the trouble of opening the door, though? Why meet us personally? If he wanted us killed or arrested, he would send a group of Praetorians to meet us here, before we ever got outside.

“Well, what are you waiting for?” asks Damian.

We step through the hatch and into the outside world.
