# Of Warmth
### Ambrosia

I make my way to the dungeons, my hand wrapped around the bottle in my bag.

There is no going back after I do this.

As my footsteps echo against the walls of the Palace, I try not to imagine how this could go wrong.

Soon, I find myself face-to-face with Grunwald, the only guard currently on duty in the dungeons. Given that the coronation is taking place as we speak, all but one of the guards have been called.

It’s not like the prisoners can just walk out.

I feel guilty for using my herbalist knowledge this way, but this is the only path available. I will escape with Adam, and we will live out our days somewhere else—anywhere but here.

“Hello, sweetie,” he says, flashing his rotting teeth.

“Good evening,” I say, performing a small curtsy.

I need to be convincing.

“I heard you were all alone tonight, and so I brought you something.”

“For me?” he asks, his eyes widening.

Reaching into my bag, I pull out the bottle.

“What is this?” he asks, as he reaches for it.

“It is something to help you relax, maybe make up for the fact that you were not invited to the coronation,” I say, wondering if I went too far with the last comment.

His eyes narrow at me, but he unscrews the top of the bottle and sniffs the drink.

“What’s in it?” he asks.

“Well,” I say, placing my hand on his arm, “it’s a surprise; from me, to you.”

I look up at him, eyes bright, hoping he takes the bait.

He grins at me and brings the bottle up to his lips before he stops himself.

I hold my breath, unsure of what is about to happen.

“This isn’t anything alcoholic, is it? I need to be alert this evening.” He sounds conflicted.

“No,” I say. “It is a type of herbal tea, meant to relax your muscles after working so hard all day.”

“You first.”

He clearly does not believe me.

Grabbing the bottle, I take a swig with no hesitation. I considered this eventuality and took an antidote, but it does not have a one-hundred percent efficacy rate. By drinking from it, I take the risk of falling asleep as well and waking up in a cell.

“Now you,” I say. I gently wipe the tea from my lips with the hem of my dress.

Smiling, he takes the bottle and empties it in one go. As he collapses off his stool, I grab onto the bottle, making sure it doesn’t break.

He should be asleep for a while.

I grab the golden ring from his finger.

* * *

### Adam

I am unable to sleep. Anxiety grips me by the throat, shaking me awake each time I doze off. As my eyes close, I see a vision of a young woman, lying suspended in red.

Is it Ambrosia? What does this mean? Why is she red? Is she bleeding? This vision is different from the one I have had prior, where she’s running towards me, drenched in blood.

Is this the aftermath? Am I watching her sleep the endless sleep, dead, before our life together can even begin?

I wake up, my heart pounding, as I wipe the sweat from my brow. In the distance, I can hear soft footsteps.

Is it her?

Has her plan worked?

As she walks through the force field, I feel a weight lifted from my shoulders.

She’s here, and she’s alive; she made it.

I get up from my cot and wrap my arms around her.

“You’re here,” I whisper.

“Yes,” she replies, leaning her head on my chest. “We have to hurry.”

She extends her open palm to me, and I take the golden ring, placing it on my index finger. We walk through the forcefield, onto the main corridor, and now, I am once again almost free.

I see Grunwald lying on the floor, sleeping peacefully.

“What did you do?” I ask.

“Gave him something. He’ll be asleep for a while, but we need to go. The ceremony has started.”

I crouch next to the man who questioned me only earlier that day, and I take his wristwatch.

Now that I have a closer look at the watch, I notice that it feels slower, clunkier. The technology feels somewhat dated.

As I wrap my hand around Grunwald’s weapon, I try to get a feel for it. Although it looks like an Atomizer, it is not as heavy, and it’s likely not as lethal, although I’m sure it’s dangerous enough.

Looking at the side, I see the stun-vaporize switch is missing.

I strap the watch to my wrist and activate it. I can see a map of the dungeons, empty apart from us three.

Three dots, meaning that Sylvan did not make it. As my eyes focus on the watch, I remember the creatures I had seen prior.

They’re likely around, instead of attending the ceremony, which means they likely do not show up on the wristwatch.

This complicates things.

“Is everything alright?” Ambrosia asks.

“Earlier today, I saw these creatures in the hallways. The wristwatch only shows us in this wing of the palace. It is unlikely that they’re at the ceremony, and thus, they must be invisible to the watch.”

“The mutts?” she asks.

“Is that what they’re called? I think so, yes,” I reply.

“I have seen them, but they’ve never attacked me before,” she says. “However, I’ve lived at the Palace my entire life. They likely recognize my scent as being familiar.”

“Which means that they will not recognize mine,” I say. “We have no choice, we have to push forward.”

“Right,” she says, nodding her head. She’s ready to follow me, even if I lead her to our death.

Looking down at Grunwald, I get an idea. He’s much larger than me, but a guard uniform would make me a lot less conspicuous, especially in the dark.

“Help me take his uniform off,” I say. Ambrosia crouches down and begins slipping his boots off, as I take off his jacket.

The uniform sags around me, although I’m hoping nobody will notice once we’re outside.

Weapon in hand, I signal Ambrosia to follow me as we begin our escape.

“Stay behind me,” I say to her, “but let me know which way is likely to have fewer mutts.”

She nods as her hand goes up, indicating the way.

We begin our descent from the floor that contains the holding cells, my heart pounding. What would happen if we encountered a mutt? I know I’d have to shoot it, which might alert people.

If I try to wrestle it with my bare hands, that would potentially make less noise, but it also carries a high likelihood of getting severely injured. What if we encounter more than one at a time?

I try not to think about all the dreadful possibilities, as we continue down the maze of corridors, Ambrosia quietly pointing the way. Before we go down one corridor or another, we stop, as I carefully peek into it, making sure there’s nothing there.

This is too easy, I think to myself, a knot beginning to form in my stomach, and that’s when I see it. A mutt turns a corner, only a few feet away, its red eyes staring at us.

I can feel Ambrosia freeze behind me. “I’ll protect you, no matter what,” I whisper to her.

My attention turns back to the mutt. Why has it not moved? Our eyes lock. As my head cocks to the side, the mutt imitates me.

“What’s going on?” Ambrosia asks.

“I don’t know. Why is it not attacking us?” I ask.

“I honestly don’t know,” she replies.

The mutt makes a move, slowly making its way to us on all fours. My hands are wrapped around the weapon, finger on the trigger, ready to shoot. The creature doesn’t seem to register the threat, and instead, calmly closes the gap between us.

It looks up at me, its head inches away from the weapon, nose twitching.

“What is it doing?” I ask.

“It’s sniffing you,” Ambrosia says.

“Why?”

As the question leaves my lips, I immediately understand.

“The ring,” I whisper.

“What?”

“The ring,” I echo, my voice bouncing off the walls.

Having sniffed the ring, the mutt loses interest and wanders off, disappearing down a corridor, now far behind us.

“Of course,” Ambrosia says. “The ring signifies that you belong here. The mutts must work similarly to the force fields. If they catch you in the corridor without one, they attack.”

I nod my head, understanding. “This is why there were so few guards. They knew that even if someone got away, they’d be ripped apart by the mutts.”

“That means we’re safe from them,” Ambrosia says.

“Maybe,” I reply. “Let’s be cautious and try to avoid them anyway, just in case.”

She nods and points towards the next corridor. “This should lead us out of the palace and onto the grounds.”

I look down at my wristwatch, checking for lifesigns. Even though most of them are congregated near the other side of the Palace, where the outdoor ceremony is taking place, there are still some guards patrolling the grounds.

“Even if the mutts don’t attack us, that won’t be the case for the guards,” I say, looking at her dress. It’s bright blue, with flowery patterns all over.

Looking down, she realizes what I’m thinking, and says, “Sorry, dresses wear only outfits like this.”

I nod, looking down at my wristwatch. I wonder how long Grunwald will be unconscious.

As the lifesigns move around, a pattern seems to emerge, presenting us with a potential path out.

The guards on patrol seem to avoid the general vicinity of the coronation event, perhaps assuming that those present are keeping an eye out.

I look down at myself, glad that I took Grunwald’s uniform.

“We’ll have to get close to the coronation,” I say, holding Ambrosia’s gaze.

“Why?”

“The patrols are tight, but there is a gap, right around the coronation event. If we can walk through that gap, we can make it into the forest. Let’s hope nobody looks too closely at my uniform.”

She nods. I put the weapon away in its holster, hoping I don’t have to use it. We’ll still have to be careful, avoiding being seen, but as long as we walk around as if we belong, people shouldn’t give us much attention.

We step outside, following the edge of the Palace, as we brazenly walk towards the large group of people congregated outside.

As we approach, I wonder why it’s held here and not inside the Palace.

“You,” I hear a voice.

I freeze as I feel Ambrosia hold her breath behind me. I stop myself from reaching for my weapon, as using it here would be madness. We are less than three hundred feet from the congregation, and a blast would cause the entire Palace guard to run towards us.

I turn around to face the man who just called me out. His features are hard to make out in the dark, which means mine must also.

“What are you doing with this dress?” he asks, pointing at Ambrosia.

“Apologies, sir. I found her walking around the grounds. She would not respond to my questions until I shook her. It turns out she was sleepwalking.”

His eyes wander over to Ambrosia, her shoulders slumped, hands clasped in front of her.

“I’m so sorry, sir, I didn’t mean to cause any trouble, especially on a night like this.”

“This young man was just escorting me back to the dormitories,” she adds, as she indicates to a wing of the Palace all the way past the congregation.

“We were trying to go around, so as not to cause any disturbance, sir.”

His eyes focus back at me, and then at my uniform.

“Who gave you this attire? You look disgraceful.”

“I’m sorry, sir,” I say, lowering my head, “I’ll make sure to get a better-fitting uniform, sir.”

He takes a few steps towards me, his dark eyes searching my face.

“You’d better. If I catch you out like this a second time, that’ll be ten lashes. Understood?”

“Yes, sir,” I say, my head lowered.

“Now escort this dress back, and make sure no one sees you dressed like this,” he adds, spitting on the ground.

“Understood,” I say, my heart jumping out of my chest. As we walk away from him, following the edge of the crowd, my hands go numb. We got lucky.

Really lucky.

In the periphery of my vision, I can see the coronation taking place. The new Imperatrix is standing in front of her throne, addressing the audience. A woman stands next to her, dressed in black, soft waves of golden light passing over her clothing.

“That’s the Mater,” says Ambrosia. “She’s the old Imperatrix, the mother of the new one.”

The Imperatrix sits down on her throne, as I realize how little I know about this world. The Mater walks over to her.

Her hands lift the crown, hovering over the head of her daughter for a moment, before lowering it in place.

The moment the crown touches the new Imperatrix, her entire being lights up like the sun, turning night into day. I feel myself hit the ground, dazzled by the light.

This must be the reason why they do it outside, at night, I think, as I stumble up to my feet.

“You!” I hear someone shout.

I grab Ambrosia’s hand, and we start running towards the forest.

“After them,” cries out a man’s voice, followed by the deafening sounds of Atomizers.

The edge of the forest is only a few feet away, inside of which we have a chance to get lost and escape. In the panic, I don’t hear the sound of the truck, as it speeds towards us from the left, cutting our path.

“Down on the ground,” screams one of the guards, as a hand wraps around my neck, pushing my head against the grass. My hand is still tightly wrapped around Ambrosia’s, as they try to cuff me.

“She had nothing to do with this,” I cry out, as they grab her too. “I kidnapped her, in case I needed to make my way out.”

I look at her as she peers into my eyes. Her face is peaceful, although she is crying. My vision goes blurry as one of the guards hits me in the head with his Atomizer. I’m fairly certain Ambrosia mouthed a soft thank you right before I find myself being dragged back up on my feet.

As the guards force me towards the back of the truck, my head begins to ache, a loud sound permeating through it.

No, no, not in front of her.

I wish I had my capsules on me. Could this be another episode? It feels somewhat familiar, but strangely different, unlike any other attack I’ve had before.

I force my eyes open, and for a split second, having approached us, I’m certain the Mater is staring at me through her veil.

“There is no need to be concerned, Holy Mater, the threat has been contained,” says a guard near me, as my body is hoisted up in the back of the truck.

“Is this the man from the Protectorate?” she asks.

“Yes,” he answers.

“Make sure he is unharmed. I wish to speak with him upon his return to the dungeon.”

“Understood, your Brightness.”

Why does she want to speak with me? What more could I possibly tell her?

Suddenly, I can feel Ambrosia’s warmth to my side, as she leans her head onto my shoulder, and I realize that I don’t know what to say.

She’s looking up at me, smiling through her tears.

“I’m sorry,” I say, knowing that my words ring hollow.

“You have nothing to feel sorry for,” she says.

She reaches up, fighting her bindings, and kisses me on the cheek. “You have saved me from the Life Tanks. With a bit of luck, we’ll die together.”

I smile, lowering my head onto hers. I know that I’m about to die, but I’m glad I tried something, anything, before they took that chance away from me. Bittersweet feelings flood my chest as I learn that she is not angry with me.

If we are to die, we’ll die together.
