# Fear
### Damian, Twenty-One Years Earlier

The mayor looks at me with an impressed look.

“Very good, Damian. Yes, if they are indeed at fault, they’ll have a vaccine ready. They wouldn’t want their royal family to be wiped out, now would they?”

With visible effort, the mayor stands up. “Let’s go.”

“Now?” asks Octavian.

“Yes, now. Come,” he moans, as he waddles over to a side door in his office. Opening it reveals an elevator. He steps inside, signaling us to join him.

The elevator starts going up, leading us into a long maintenance corridor. As we step out of the elevator, the door that closes behind us indicates that this is a janitor’s closet.

The mayor searches through his pockets, exclaiming “Aha,” once he finds what he is looking for. His hands shaking, he enters a large key into an even larger lock, stepping through the door and onto the platform.

“Who will we negotiate with?” I ask as we approach the train.

“Whilst it is difficult to know if we are right about them being desperate, you will likely negotiate directly with the Imperatrix and the Mater,” says the mayor.

“The what?” asks Octavian.

“Through their mastery of genetics, the High Ones have not only obtained the ability to see thoughts, but also to live very long lives. Because of this, laws were established centuries ago, limiting terms for each Imperatrix to twenty years.”

As we approach the train, the doors open automatically, and we step through.

“At the age of twenty,” continues the mayor, “a Princess becomes an Imperatrix, and the previous Imperatrix becomes a Mater, an advisor. At the starting point of her rule, the Imperatrix must give birth to a daughter, who will, in twenty years, take her place as Imperatrix.”

“What happens to the Maters?” I ask.

“I’m not sure,” says the mayor. “I’m not even sure all of this information is still correct.”

As the train moves, the mayor instructs us, “Outside, you will find a vehicle. Once you’re inside, it will automatically take you to the Palace.”

“What if they take it, or try to reverse engineer it?” I ask.

The mayor shakes his head. “The vehicle has been programmed to only respond to either of your genetic signatures. Nobody else can operate it. If someone attempts to take it apart and reverse engineer it, the batteries are rigged to overheat and explode.”

“So that’s it? We just go in like this? We’re completely unprepared,” says Octavian.

The mayor reaches into his jacket and pulls out a device, giving it to us.

“This device will provide you with more information; specifics of what you are to negotiate, and what types of technology you are authorized to give. Much like the vehicle, it is coded with your genetics, so it will only respond to you two.”

The mayor coughs and then continues, “Remember, the Imperatrix, as well as the Mater, can see your thoughts. We do not know precisely how deep they can go, or how clearly they can read them, but we must assume that they have perfect clarity. Anything else would be foolishness.”

We nod our heads.

“This device has been built with that in mind. On your way to the Palace, speak to her, and she will answer back. She will give you enough to begin negotiations, which will likely take place in multiple rounds. As the negotiations progress, she will be able to instruct you, using all of the knowledge that we have on the High Ones, and that which she gathers during your trip. She will be listening.”

“She?” echoes Octavian.

The mayor smiles, “The voice of the machine is modeled after a woman who lived a very long time ago, or so I’m told. Eve has always been here, since the start of the Protectorate.”

“Now,” he continues, “at the end of each round of negotiations, confer with the machine. If the conditions are met, and you have reached the next stage of negotiations, she will unlock more information for you and a new strategy. This way, you will be able to keep secrets from the High Ones; secrets which you do not know, as you have yet to learn them.”

The mayor clears his throat and continues, “It goes without saying that if the High Ones attempt to force you to show them the contents of the devices, the negotiations are forfeit.”

“And our lives are likely as well,” says Octavian.

“Not necessarily,” says the mayor solemnly.

“How come?” I ask.

“Inside the vehicle, we have included a weapons chest. You will likely not be allowed weapons in the Palace; however, should you find yourselves in trouble, provided you can get to the vehicle, you will stand a chance.”

The train arrives at the far platform, and we disembark. Operating the computer terminal next to the door, the mayor opens the hatch, and so, the two of us step outside.
