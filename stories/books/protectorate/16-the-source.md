# The Source
### Adam, Present Day

I approach one of the pods, shocked at how similar it looks to the ones in the Protectorate. They are not identical, but the underlying technology is clearly very similar.

Looking inside, I become confused by its occupant. It looks like a brother, although unlike any that I have ever seen before. He has long locks, but no facial hair, and his features are very soft.

As my eyes peer down, I notice that his chest is lumpy and elongated, and his abdomen is distended.

“Could the radiation have caused this?” I ask.

Sylvan is examining the brother in the pod next to me. “There is no radiation. At least not any significant amount.”

My eyes wander down below the swollen abdomen, to the brother’s pelvic area, my eyes widening in shock.

“They removed his manhood,” I cry out.

“What?” spits Sylvan, examining his pod more closely. “I can’t believe it. You’re right, they did. Are the surface dwellers animals?”

V and Octavian are examining the occupants of two other pods, too shocked to speak.

“Is it possible that these are not brothers?” I ask.

“What do you mean?” asks V.

“Can they be a different species? One that we do not know about, from before the event?”

V rubs his chin, deep in thought. “It is theoretically possible,” he says.

Suddenly, my heart sinks as I hear a familiar sound. A distant whirring, causing my body to be inundated with fear. It can’t be, Lumina drones only exist in the Protectorate.

The whirring has stopped.

I turn my head back towards my pod, looking at it closely. Why is this man’s abdomen swollen in such a manner? Is he sick? Is this a hospital, and are they all receiving treatment?

Suddenly, I remember. I have had a dream about a creature not too dissimilar to this. It did not have a swollen belly, but its chest was also lumpy. What does this mean?

“Why aren’t they here by now?” I hear V say.

“Who?” I ask, as I turn my head.

“What?” says V.

“You asked why they aren’t here by now,” I repeat. “Who are you talking about?”

V is visibly shocked.

“He did not say that,” interjects Sylvan.

My brow furrows as I try to make out what has just occurred. I clearly heard V say that. Why did the others not hear him? Why did Octavian not hear him? He’s right next to V.

My eyes dart around, looking for him.

“Octavian?” I ask. “Where did he go?”

A flash, coming from somewhere on the floor, blinds me. In the confusion that follows, I find myself on the ground, my hands bound together.

I can hear men shouting, orders being barked. “Make sure you get all of them!”

Not again.

What is happening? Why can I not see?

Two men grab me and lift me to my feet. As I blink, some of my sight begins to return, and I see a tall man standing in front of me, accompanied by what I think is a person.

At least, I think it was a person a long time ago. The thing that crouches over me looks more like a monster, bearing its fangs, its blood-red eyes fixated on mine.

“Medic,” the tall man says, turning his head. “Scan them.“

A slender man walks over to me and flashes a small light in my eyes for a moment.

“Can someone get this mutt out of here? I need to work,“ the medic complains, stepping around the creature.

The device in his hand beeps. “They are infected. Protectorate.”

He slides the device back into his pocket and pulls a syringe out of a small medical kit.

“What is that?” I ask, trying to get loose.

The doctor approaches me, preparing the injection.

“Make sure he stays still,” he says to the soldiers. They tighten their grip.

“No,” I cry out as the doctor injects me. Walking over to Sylvan and V, he administers the same injection.

Taking out his scanner once more, he flashes it into my eyes a second time. The device gives two beeps.

“They’re clear,” the doctor says.

“Good,” replies the tall man, clearly in charge.

“Excuse me,” says V. “I demand to be released at once.”

“They won’t let us go,” Sylvan says.

“Not you,” cries V. “Me!”

My eyes widen. “These are the men you are waiting for?”

The tall man walks over to V and grabs his face with his large hand. “You V?” he asks.

He tries to shake his head free and fails, then nods. “Yes.”

The man laughs and walks around, examining us carefully.

“Gentlemen, I’m afraid I have to inform you that you have a snake in your midst. This man has notified us of your impending arrival days ago. Isn’t that right?” he grins, walking over to V.

“So what?” V spits. “I gave them up; now I demand that you let me go.”

Why would he do this? Why would he betray us?

“Why, V?” asks Sylvan, with tears in his eyes.

“You all grew up in the dirt, and you’re used to being treated like animals. I was born higher, free from many of the indignities that you had to endure, and yet, still subject to many others.”

He spits on the ground and continues, “I wanted to escape, that part is true. However, I did not want to live like this, scraping just enough food to live day by day. The truth is that I found out quite a lot from the data we extracted, far more than what I told you.”

“These men,” he continues, tilting his head towards them, “they’re the Royal Guard of the High Ones. Once I found out about them, I discovered a hidden channel in the Protectorate’s communication system. It was very well hidden, but I found it. I managed to get in touch with them and promised I’d give them all the information they wanted, plus all of you.”

V struggles pointlessly for a moment before continuing, “In exchange, I am to be given citizenship and high social status so that I may continue to live a lavish lifestyle here, above ground.”

Sylvan begins to laugh. “V, you’re the smartest man I’ve ever known, which is why I’m surprised to see how stupid you are at this very moment. I don’t know anything about these men, but I do know one thing: nobody likes a turncoat.”

“Isn’t that right?” Sylvan asks as he turns to the Head Royal Guard, who is still grinning.

“You are precisely right.”

“This is why you said we shouldn’t remove our chips,” I murmur. “It’s so that they can find us once we are above ground.”

The main guard laughs and says, “Very good. This young one’s fairly perceptive. Yes, we told him to make sure you all keep your chips. We can track them, you see,” he says as he shows us his wrist.

My eyes widen. The man is showing us a Praetorian wristwatch. How can this be? Where would he even get one?

“Wait,” cries V. “There is another man with us. I can help you find him.”

“Hmm?” says the head guard. He looks at his wristwatch, examining the search area.

“No, it’s only us, and you three. There were only three dots since we tracked you down a little while ago, after you tripped the security system.”

V’s mouth opens, but no words come out.

“Alright, time to move,” the man says, as he looks at his wrist. “Throw everyone in the wagon, and let’s go.”

As we drive away, I find myself lost in thought. I now have evidence that my auditory hallucinations are more than that. Was I able to hear V’s thoughts? It can’t be. This is madness.

What other explanation is there? The anxiety I felt from V, as we walked outside, the fact that he was expecting the guards, and countless other hallucinations throughout my life.

They are not hallucinations, but glimpses into the minds of others, but why now? Why is this manifesting now?

My stomach sinks as I come to the realization. The capsules. The very same ones that I had taken daily for my supposed panic attacks.

They were not panic attacks at all. My mind was attempting to liberate itself.

Am I losing it? Even with the mountain of evidence at my disposal, I find it hard to believe that I can read minds.

I can not tell anybody, ever. In the best-case scenario, I would be met with amusement. Worst case? I’d be dissected and studied.

Looking around, I realize I may not need to keep the secret for long. Who knows what these men plan to do with us?

I should be panicking right now, but I am not. Why? In the past, whenever I’ve had attacks, they were always preceded by moments like these. Why is my mind not on fire? Why am I not hyperventilating?

Does knowing the truth change something inside of you?
