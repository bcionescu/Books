# Wishes
### Damian

The guard knocks on our door. We start to move towards the Sun chamber, where our negotiations started, but the guard signals to follow him in the opposite direction.

“Where are we going?” asks Octavian.

“The meeting place has changed,“ responds the guard. “You are to hold the next round of discussions in a new location.”

“Where?” I ask.

“You will find out soon enough.”

We follow the guard onto a spiral staircase that seems to go on forever. Eventually, we reach the top, finding ourselves on a platform at the very top of the palace, offering us a 360-degree view of the surroundings.

My eyes widen as I walk towards the edge of the platform.

“What is that?” I ask the guard, as I point to a massive red structure, partially sunk into the ocean.

“It was called the Golden Gate Bridge, constructed long before the Cataclysm,” replies the guard, before taking his place at the other end of the platform, far from us.

To my right is a white metal table, surrounded by ornate chairs of the same color. The metal glistens in the bright summer sun.

“I see you find the view as enjoyable as I do,” says the Imperatrix, as she comes up the stairs, followed by the Mater.

“We do,” replies Octavian. “It is magnificent.“

“Yes,” says the Imperatrix. “The Palace is built on top of a place that used to be called the Presidio of San Francisco. It was quite charming back in the day, or so I’m told.”

“San Francisco?” I ask.

“It was a city in the state of California, but this was a long time ago, before the Cataclysm,” the Imperatrix says, before having a seat at the table.

Octavian and I pick up on the cue and sit down as well, although the Mater remains standing behind her daughter.

It is evident that the Imperatrix is not in full control and that the Mater has far more sway than they would have us believe. Even though she is standing and has been completely quiet for the entire negotiation process, as a former Imperatrix, she must still retain some level of power.

“In the days before the Cataclysm,” continues the Imperatrix, “the world was organised in far different ways. The Prophet Eriksson, Carrier of the Great Word of the Sun, had foreseen that the Great Solar Winds would lay waste to civilization, allowing us to build a better world from its ashes.”

I had never heard of this prophet before, and I am certain that neither has Octavian. What else has the Protectorate hidden from us?

“In his great wisdom, one of the few men of note throughout history, Eriksson’s initial prophecy was only one side of a coin. The other warns us that the Great Solar Winds are bound to return, and if we are to survive them this time, we must be wary of our dependence on technology.”

“However,” Octavian adds, “if we do not adopt some technology, we might find ourselves vulnerable to those who do.”

“That is very wise,” the Imperatrix says, smiling at him.

Why do I feel a twinge of jealousy at a brother? Why does the Imperatrix smiling at him cause this sensation in my stomach?

“As direct descendants of the Prophet Eriksson, we, the High Ones, have been tasked with the burden of carrying his legacy. However, as this bright young man pointed out, harnessing some technology to our benefit might also prove advantageous, as long as we do not over-rely on it.”

“Technology can be interesting,” she continues, “even as a curiosity. Technology can always be shared in the name of common interest.”

Here we are, finally getting to the point. They wish to share technology. Fortunately, so do we. So, let’s see what technology they respond to.

The guard approaches the table and whispers something to the Imperatrix.

“Gentlemen, I’m afraid something has come up, and we will have to suspend negotiations for today. The guard will escort you back whenever you wish.”

We stand up and bow slightly.

“Two more things. One, we require that you hand over your artificial intelligence device so that our engineers may examine it and determine whether it is a danger to us. Two, due to a burst pipe in your room, we will have to change your accommodation. Unfortunately, we do not currently have another room large enough to house both of you, and so, to maintain the luxurious standard you have enjoyed so far, we have managed to source two separate rooms for you.”

A second man walks up the stairs and stands next to the other guard.

“When you are ready to leave the platform, you will each be taken to your own rooms. Now, the device, if you will,” says the Imperatrix, as the Mater extends her hand.

I look over at Octavian, knowing that we have no choice. I take the device out of my pocket and hand it over to the Mater.

“Thank you ever so much,” says the Imperatrix, as the women walk down the stairs.

We turn around and head towards the bannister at the edge of the platform.

“We are as good as dead,” I say, taking in the view, knowing that this might be the last time I see it.

“Maybe,” says Octavian.
