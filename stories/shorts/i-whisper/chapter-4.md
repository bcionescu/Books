# Disagreement

Pouring the remainder of the instant coffee down my throat, I crumple the paper cup, and throw it into the trash.

I feel tired.

The door swings open, and Eriksson walks in, wearing cuffs. Thadeus sits him down, walks around the table, and takes his place next to me.

This is going to be difficult. The man sitting in front of me is innocent. No, not just innocent. He almost became a victim. I know this, but revealing this information would mean that the mother of my future child will die.

“Good evening, Mr. Eriksson. Could you confirm your full name for me?” I say.

“My name is Ragnar Eriksson.”

“Where is our accent from?” Thadeus asks.

Ragnar’s eyes shift in his direction.

He understands the implication.

“Norway, which, as you know, is not an ally of Japan.”

“Given what Japan has done to Norway,” I intervene, “I doubt Mr. Eriksson is a fan of the Shogunate. How did you come to the United States?”

“War visa. I am an Engineer.”

“Tell me, Mr. Eriksson, where were you last night, around 10 PM?”

“I was at a small gathering,” he replies.

“Other people who we’ve interviewed today said that your small gathering was more of a speech.”

“People can define it however they wish. Everyone in attendance can confirm where I was. Once the bombs hit, I helped tend to the wounded. The body was discovered later that evening, and the authorities were informed.”

“So you don’t know what happened?”

“No, I do not.”

“Many among the Rellies consider you a Prophet.”

“I only speak the truth,” says Ragnar.

“Dangerous thing to do,” I reply. “Don’t you know we’re at war?”

“I speak about the things I see. Some choose to listen. I did not ask to be called a Prophet, nor do I wish to become one.”

“Well, well,” Thadeus says. “Eriksson, the Unwilling Prophet.”

Ragnar says nothing.

“What was it about?” I ask.

“What?”

“Your speech last night. What was it about?”

“Absurdity.”

“How so?”

“At birth, a neuralbond implant is placed in our bodies. This technology allows us to communicate with whomever we want, at any moment, and yet, we seem to find it impossible to speak to the other side.”

“The enemy, you mean?”

Ragnar looks away, and remains silent.

“Do you not see the advantages of the neuralbond implant? Is it not a positive? Do you not recognize that this technology brings order to society, that it makes us all bound to our word?”

Eriksson’s eyes shift down to my badge.

Does he know?

“You can call me Claudius,” I say.

His eyes move back up, to meet mine.

“Claudius,” Eriksson starts, “I am not against technology. I hope this is a given, considering my profession. I believe that technology has the capacity to help humanity ascend.”

“If that is so,” I ask, “why do you argue against it? Why do you push against neuralbond technology?”

“One of the side effects of technology is that it creates social change, as well as unforeseen consequences. This happened with the introduction of the implants decades ago. Only the very wealthy were able to afford them, and thus it awarded them status.”

With his hands bound, Eriksson struggles to sip water from his paper cup, before continuing.

“They were no longer as bound by biology as everyone else. They were free to communicate with each other silently, and so, the implants became very desirable. As the newer models became less expensive, an increasing number of people could now afford them.”

“How is that a bad thing?” I ask.

Eriksson’s eyes briefly dart back down to my name tag, before continuing.

“Over time, the implants stopped being a luxury, and instead became a necessity. Now, if you do not have one, or choose not to use it, you are relegated to an underclass at best, or treated as a criminal at worst. Is it that surprising that the Rellies might be interested in what I have to say?”

“But society is better off as a whole because of this technology,” Thadeus interjects.

“Many would disagree,” says Eriksson. “Before such a technology is implemented, its deep consequences to society and culture must first be discussed.”

“Thank you, Mr. Eriksson. That will be all for now. Please remain within San Francisco until further notice.”
