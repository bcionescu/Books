I sit at my computer, open up the Terminal, and pull up the case file.

```bash
vim 2135-07-04-Kiln-Case.md
```

There has to be something in here that I have missed. A piece of the puzzle which, once in place, would make everything clear.

I review the case data once more, examining each detail. According to the logs, the data packet was created at 22:07 hours, moments before the bombs fell. Unable to find a relay, the data packet then jumped over to Sarah, where it remained for a few more hours.

At 00:12 hours, the data packet jumped to a relay near the theater, as she fled.

Suddenly, I see it. The missing piece that I had been looking for.

In a daze, I scribble down the ID, before dashing out of the building. The answer was there all along, I was too blind to see it.

I jump in my cruiser, and within minutes, I find myself knocking on Sarah’s door.

She opens up and lovingly greets me.

*I wasn’t expecting to see you so early,* she says, barely managing to wrap her arms around my chest.

*I think I have something,* I wheeze, having run up the stairs.

*What?*

*The spoofed ID that sent the data packet was yours. However, its metadata reveals that the original packet was sent to one more node.*

*Who?*

*Another spoofed ID.*

Sarah’s eyes widen as she has the same idea. *It reached one of the Rellies.*

*Yes, I think so,* I say, still breathless. *Since the other data packet has not arrived yet, it means that the person who has it is likely still at the theater. If we go there, would you be able to find who this spoofed ID belongs to?*

*Yes, however...*

*The data packet might be too decayed, yes. We still have to try.*

Sarah rushes back into her apartment, and grabs a small device from her desk.

*Let’s go,* she says, as we rush downstairs to my cruiser.

*No,* Sarah says. *We can’t show up in a police vehicle.*

That makes sense. The Rellies would likely not be pleased to see that, so I hail a taxi instead.

Minutes later, we arrive at the theater, and rush inside.

Sarah pulls out the device, and starts scanning the rooms. *If they’re here, we’ll find them.*

As we walk around, I can feel the gazes of Rellies hiding in the shadows.

*I can’t find the spoofed ID,* says Sarah, as we find our way back to the foyer. *I think they may not be here.*

Looking around, I spot a half-open door.

I remember the young girl, crying, holding onto her rag doll.

*The girl,* I say.

*Which one?*

*The one with the doll. You said that you spoofed her ID right before Kiln showed up?*

Sarah’s eyes widen. *I haven’t seen her.*

*Have we checked every single room?*

*I think so,* Sarah says, looking around.

We look up at the same time.

Rushing up the stairs breathlessly, we reach the top floor, and gently open the door to the attic.

Something scurries in the dark.

*Hello?* I ask.

As my eyes adjust to the darkness, residual light from a far window reveals a small shape cowering behind a large wooden box. I signal Sarah, and show her where the girl is.

*It’s OK,* Sarah says, as she approaches the girl. *What is your name?*

*Mika-chan,* the girl whispers.

I approach cautiously, as I try not to scare her.

*Mika-chan,* I whisper, *what do you have there?*

I point at her doll.

*It’s a Teru Teru Bōzu doll,* says Sarah. *It means shine-shine monk. It is a prayer for good weather.*

*In San Francisco?*

*Kumo-san, inaku narimasu yō ni.*

*What did she say?* I ask.

*So that the clouds may go away,* Sarah replies.

It was never about the weather. It was about the black clouds from the bombings. It was about her parents dying, just like mine, to this war.

*It’s really nice outside. How about we take you out for a treat?* whispers Sarah.
