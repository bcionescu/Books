# Answers

As I enter Sarah’s apartment, I can hear her gently breathing in the bedroom. I make my way to the bed, and gently climb in, so as not to wake her.

*Hello,* she whispers, as I gently slide my arm underneath her warm body. 

*Sorry. I didn’t mean to wake you.*

*That’s OK,* she says, as she pulls herself closer to me. *Any updates?*

*As expected, Eriksson said that he was holding a gathering. I assume that is true.*

Sarah nods.

*We’re no closer to finding out what actually happened. There is one thing that bothers me, though.*

*What?*

*The person who passed along the last transmission from Kiln had a spoofed ID. I assume it was you?*

*Very likely, yes.*

*Why is the data corrupted? Would a blast cause this?*

*Partially,* she replies. *When a relay is absent, and a data packet is sent to a nearby person, it may become stuck. If a person spends too much time away from a relay, idle data packets begin to decay. Within a day or so, any packets that have not had any more jumps expire.*

*So you did not immediately flee after Kiln died?*

*No, I was scared. I hid in an empty part of the building for hours. When I heard that the body was found, I fled.*

*So your data packet from Kiln started to decay, as it could not find a relay or another node to jump to.*

*Yes. That likely did the majority of the damage.*

Suddenly, it came to me.

*What’s the matter?* Sarah asks, seeing my expression.

*It is clearly possible to spoof IDs. Could it also be possible to spoof data packets?*

*Theoretically yes, but it is highly unlikely.*

*How so?*

*When data packets jump to a relay, or from node to node, they are encrypted in transit.*

*Can these data packets be intercepted by someone like you?*

*Yes,* Sarah replies, *but the encryption is unbreakable. The only two entities with the key are either the police, or...*

*Or the company that created the neuralbond to begin with.*

Sarah pulls herself nearer to me. *Do you think that is how Ichika was going to be framed?*

*I think so, yes. Kiln was going to kill Eriksson, and he would have made sure there were witnesses. When the Rellies would have rushed to a relay to report the crime, the company would have intercepted the packets, and modified them, in order to portray Ichika as the murderer.*

*Their neuralspeek would have counted for more than their testimony.*

*Yes,* I confirm.

*What will you do?* she asks.

*I need to speak to the company director,* I say, as I slide out of the bed.

*Isn’t it late?* she asks, as she looks at the time.

*He’ll see me,* I whisper.

A minute later I am outside, hailing a taxi.

Historians always thought that by now cash would be banned, or at least phased out. It turns out that when you live through constant conflicts, having cash on hand is always preferable.

This ensured that cash stuck around. This is useful when you want to get a taxi in the middle of the night, but you don’t want your trip recorded.

Normally I’d have to provide ID, but seeing the bundle of paper credits, the driver takes me to my destination without asking any questions.

The security guard intercepts me as I approach the gates.

“This is private property,” he says, his right hand on his holstered weapon.

“Tell your boss Claudius is here to speak with him.”

The security guard looks me over, before relaxing, and returning to his post.

A minute later, the gates open, and the guard indicates that I can walk through.

“Claudius, my boy,” says the director, as he walks down the stairs of his mansion. “Isn’t it a bit late for a friendly visit? I would have happily seen you in the morning.”

“My apologies, this could not wait.”

The director’s expression darkens slightly. My senses tell me something is wrong.

“OK, what can I do for you?”

“Is it possible to spoof neuralbond data packets?”

He looks taken aback by the question.

“My dear boy, is this why you’ve come to my house in the middle of the night?”

He’s hiding something.

“Is it possible,” I insist, “to spoof neuralbond data packets.”

“Look, Claudius, as the heir of Blackthorne Industries, you have the right to ask me questions about the day to day business operations. You can inquire how much money any division is making, what our costs are, etc. That being said, you are asking about sensitive trade secrets. When your parents passed away at the start of the war, this was made very clear to you.”

So, yes. They can be spoofed.

“I understand. Thank you. I shan’t take any more of your time.”

After shaking the hand of the bewildered director, I turn around, and walk back out.
