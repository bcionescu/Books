I look out over the Golden Gate bridge, slumped into the water, as Mika-chan runs around us happily. Occasionally, she stops to look at her newborn baby brother, before running off again, laughing happily.

Picking up the newspaper, I once again read the headline.

“Neuralbond technology likely to be banned, Government officials say”

Once we brought Mika close to a relay, the data packet sitting in her neuralbond implant made its way to police servers. While the data was partially decayed, our technicians managed to put together the remaining bits of the puzzle.

The director of Blackthorne Industries had hired Kiln to murder Eriksson, due to the ever-growing numbers of followers that he was gathering. His rhetoric was impacting profits, and so, he had to be taken out of the picture.

As we learnt about packet spoofing, the Government understood the dangers that it presented. All of a sudden, anyone that had access to the decryption keys would be able to counterfeit any data packet, rendering the technology unreliable.

Smiling, I feel the paper in my hands. Such publications are once again becoming popular, as a counterculture movement against neuralbond.

For decades, we have been served our news wirelessly. I suppose people had begun to crave for the good old days.

Whilst whispering is still technically illegal, people have begun to do it in public. The taboo is being lifted, and soon enough, it might once again become the norm to speak to one another.

Maybe our children have a chance at growing up in a normal world, after all.

I look up from my paper, and onto the remains of the bridge.

Who knows when they’ll ever rebuild it?

*One thing at a time,* I whisper.
