# Surprise

I knock on the door.

Within moments, it swings open, and Sarah wraps herself around me.

*I’ve heard about what happened today. I’m happy you’re safe.*

*Not here,* I whisper.

I place my hand on her back, and gently lead her inside, as I close the door behind us.

*I’ve already had dinner at the station. It was a long day. I just want to talk.*

Sarah smiles, grabs my wrist with both hands, and leads me to her bed. I lay down, exhausted, as she cuddles up to me, resting her head on my chest.

*What happened? Do they know who did it?* she asks, as she reaches for a small box of painkillers on her bedside table.

*Headache?*

*A mild one,* she whispers, as she throws her head back, swallowing the pill with a mouthful of water. *So, what’s the scoop?*

*I hope you’re not asking as a reporter,* I smile.

Sarah rolls her eyes back and punches me playfully in the shoulder. *You know I’d never spill.*

I smile, but I feel worried. My cop instincts are telling me something, but what?

*We’re not sure what happened. We know Kiln died at the same time as the bombing last night. He has numerous wounds, so the coroner will need some time to examine his body.*

*Where was he?*

*At the Rellie compound, in the abandoned theater near Golden Gate.* I pause for a moment, as I run my fingers over her bare arm, stopping at the small birthmark on the back of her hand; a semicircle underneath two of her knuckles, forming a shape that resembles a smiling face.

*You know,* I continue, *I worry that you spend so much time there.*

*You know I have to.*

*Today, we had Ichika in for questioning. We haven’t found Eriksson yet, but we’re looking for him. During the interview, my colleague asked her why she does this. Why does she choose to live with the Rellies, when she doesn’t look like them?.*

*What did she say?* whispers Sarah.

*She said that she is Japanese, and thus, she feels that she needs to help them.*

I pause for a moment.

*Do you feel the same?*

Sarah lifts her head up to face me.

I brush the ginger hair away from her almond-shaped eyes, the only indication that she is not entirely European.

Something is wrong.

*What’s the matter?* I ask. I can feel my breathing accelerate.

*No-nothing,* she manages to say, before gripping the sides of her head.

“I did it. I think I did it. I think I’m responsible.”

I look at Sarah, unsure of how I should respond.

She just admitted to killing Kiln. That much is clear. The painkillers earlier, the headache, the feeling that something was off. I should have pieced it all together.

Sarah begins to cry in my arms.

I grip her tight, and allow her to cry until she is spent, and breathing easier. A neuralspill is no joking matter, however, when combined with a confession such as this, anyone would break down.

*It’s OK,* I say, as I run my hand through her hair. *Tell me what happened.*

She looks up at me, eyes puffy and red.

*You know you can trust me. Just tell me what happened.*

*At the compound, there’s this shy girl. Her parents died in a bombing recently, so she’s been orphaned. I’ve been spoofing the neuralbond IDs of some of the Rellies, at their request, to make them less trackable. I’ve been trying to do hers for a while, but she kept running away from me. She’s always hiding, holding on to her doll.*

She pauses for a moment to breathe, before continuing.

*Last night, I managed to convince her to let me spoof her ID. Once I was done, she ran off. As I stood up, a man walked into the room. He looked poorly, and so I walked up to him, asking if he needed help. That’s when I heard it.*

*He was experiencing neuralflooding,* I whisper.

*Yes.*

*What did he spill?*

*He said that he has to kill Eriksson, and that he needs to frame Ichika.*

*She’s his number two, so she would make for a reasonable suspect,* I admit.

Sarah nods, and continues.

*He started running towards me, whispering that he would kill me.*

*He didn’t want that to be recorded, so he whispered it.*

*That’s when it happened. A shock wave blew out the windows, followed by a deafening sound. I’m not quite sure what happened. I think I pushed him, but the next thing I know, I was on the floor, looking at him. I stood up, but he did not move, and so I ran.*

Even if I take this to the station, they would ask me how I know this information. Who gave it to me? It doesn’t matter if she’s guilty or not. He’s dead, and she would be executed, guilty or not.

Why was Kiln there? Why would he want to kill Eriksson, and how would he frame Ichika?

*There is one other thing I was going to tell you, but I’m scared. I don’t know how you’ll react.*

I give Sarah a kiss on her forehead.

*I don’t think you can surprise me with anything else after that,* I say, smiling.

*How do you feel about becoming a father?* she asks, burying her face in my chest.

I was wrong.

She could still surprise me.

Tucking two fingers underneath her chin, I lift up her face.

*I will do everything in my power to protect you and our unborn child. Do you trust me?*

*I do.*

Thadeus’s neuralvoice fills my mind.

“We got Eriksson.”

“Now?”

“Yes. He’s on the way to the station.”

*What’s happening?* Sarah asks.

*They have Eriksson. I need to go.*

I kiss Sarah, hug her once more, throw my uniform back on, and head down the stairs.
