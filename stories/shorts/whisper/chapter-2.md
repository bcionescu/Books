# The Interrogation

“State your name,” says Thadeus.

*Ichika Carrington.*

My head whips over to the woman standing across from us.

“You do know that fleshspeek is punishable by law?” Thadeus replies.

“My apologies, sir. I am but a simple Rellie. My name is Ichika Carrington,” the woman replies using neuralspeek.

I try not to smile.

Clearing his throat, Thadeus continues, “Ichika Carrington, born in Osaka to a father descended from English merchants, and a half-Japanese mother who died giving birth.”

Even after decades of conditioning, people still sometimes instinctively clear their throat before communicating mentally.

“Yes, sir. That is correct,” replies Ichika with a polite smile.

“Where were you last night, around 10 PM?” Thadeus continues.

“I was attending a speech held by the Prophet.”

“Eriksson?”

Ichika nods.

“Who can vouch for you?”

“Everyone in attendance.”

“The word of a Rellie doesn’t carry much weight, I’m afraid.”

Ichika shrugs, maintaining her smile.

“Can I ask you something?” Thadeus continues. “Off the record.”

“Certainly.”

“You were born in a wealthy family. Despite the fact that you are partially Japanese, if I had to guess, I’d say you were European. Why do you do this to yourself? Why associate with Rellies?”

My eyes turn to Ichika.

I can sense deathly hatred behind her ever-present smile.

“Many of the Rellies are Japanese, just like me. I may not look like it, but I am. I carry the legacy of my bloodline, both European, and Japanese. The fact that I can hide, and pretend like Rellies are not suffering, does not mean that it would be the right thing to do.”

“They are my people,” Ichika concludes.

Thadeus examines the file in front of him for a few moments, before continuing, “Thank you for your time, Miss Carrington. You are free to leave the station, however, we would ask that you not leave San Francisco for the time being.”

Thadeus stands up, and escorts her out.
